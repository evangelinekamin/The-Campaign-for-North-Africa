## Part A — Rule 12.46, verbatim (PDF p.020)

> **[12.46] Trucks may be affected by Artillery Fire.** Every time a Player fires at a given target, be
> it Infantry, Armor or whatever, he must roll a second time to determine whether any Trucks attached to
> the Target unit and/or its parent unit (if any) are affected. Thus, if a Player has 10 Barrage Points
> firing at Infantry he rolls once for the effect on the target and if, and there are any Trucks in the
> same hex as the unit being barraged then the Barraging Player rolls again on the 10 point row under
> Trucks to see how many, if any Trucks were destroyed. The choice of Trucks to be removed is up to the
> Defender, but losses must be distributed as evenly as possible among types of trucks and types of
> cargo. Players should note that when barraging Infantry, if those TOE points are already in Trucks
> (motorized) those Trucks are also eliminated, in addition to any Trucks actually noted on the Artillery
> Barrage Table.

(The "and if, and there are" is the book's own grammar, reproduced verbatim — not an OCR artefact; I
read it directly off the scan.)

Supporting context, also verbatim off PDF p.020, needed to implement 12.46 correctly:

> **[12.41]** The *Artillery Barrage Table* (12.6) is a CRT that covers artillery fire against
> personnel, Armor, Artillery and **concurrent effects on Trucks**. Regardless of the target (see Case
> 12.5) the Player will use the Artillery Barrage Table for his results.
>
> **[12.42]** The Artillery Barrage Table uses a two-dice sequential read-out; i.e., a "3" and a "4" are
> 34, reading the larger die first. These die rolls comprise the columns of the Table, while the total
> Barrage Points applied to the Target comprise the rows of the table. [...]
>
> **[12.43]** The Player notes the number of Barrage Points being fired, adjusts the column left if
> necessary and rolls the two dice. He then cross-references those two numbers under the correct target
> type (personnel, tanks, or artillery) and obtains the result. [Continues, PDF p.020 col.1, last
> sentence of the Procedure block:] **The Player then repeats the process to determine whether any
> trucks in the hex are affected.**
>
> **[12.44]** If a result of "P" is obtained, the entire target unit is "Pinned". [...] "Pin" results
> from Barrage **do not occur against Gun-class units** or Artillery HQ units.
>
> **[12.45]** A number as a result (e.g., 1 or 2) indicates the number of TOE Strength Points are
> destroyed by the Barrage. Casualties are taken after all Barrages are completed.

### Reading 12.46 as a procedure

1. The Barraging Player fires at a primary target of a known class (Infantry/Armor/Gun — see 12.22,
   which lists only these three classes as biddable primary targets; **Trucks are never a primary
   Barrage target**, only ever the secondary 12.46 check below).
2. He rolls **one d66** against the primary target's row/class on [12.6], banded by the same **Actual
   Barrage Points** column used for the primary roll.
3. If — and only if — there are Trucks physically in the same hex, "attached to the Target unit and/or
   its parent unit," he rolls a **second, independent d66** against the **same Actual-Barrage-Points
   column**, this time read off the **Truck** row of [12.6]. (Same fire, same Barrage Points, no
   additional Ammo or CP charged — this is a second CRT lookup of the same barrage, not a second attack.)
4. **Separately**, if the primary roll's result fell on motorized Infantry (Infantry TOE points already
   riding in Trucks), those specific carrying Trucks are eliminated too — **in addition to** whatever
   step 3 destroys. This is a third, independent source of truck loss triggered off the *primary*
   Infantry result, not off the Truck-row roll.
5. Defender chooses which Trucks are removed, "distributed as evenly as possible among types of trucks
   and types of cargo" (54.2's Light/Medium/Heavy typing and whatever Ammo/Fuel/Stores/Water/Inf/Arty/AA
   cargo they carry).

### Interpretation notes (judgement calls, not book contradictions — flagged per instructions)

- The Key's "1"/"2" wording is generic across all four target classes and literally says "TOE Strength
  Point" even for Guns and Trucks, neither of which the book elsewhere measures in TOE Strength Points
  (Guns have TOE Strength Points as a unit stat, so that phrase is literal for Guns; **Trucks are
  denominated in Truck Points**, 54.2). Reading "Guns or Trucks: Lose one TOE Strength Point" as "Trucks:
  lose one **Truck Point**" is the only reading consistent with rule 12.46 itself ("how many, if any
  Trucks were destroyed") and with the analogous 41.5 Key ("Trucks: that number of Truck Points... "). I
  transcribe the chart's literal wording above and record this substitution as the intended reading, not
  as a resolved ambiguity — an implementer should treat "Truck row result N" = "N Truck Points lost."
- **OWNER RULING NEEDED — scaling of the Infantry-in-Trucks rider on a "2" result.** The asterisk
  ("*If the Infantry is in Trucks, eliminate one Truck Point") is attached only to the Infantry row's
  "1" result; the "2" result says only "As above, except lose two points" without repeating or scaling
  the truck-elimination clause. Two readings are both defensible from the printed text alone:
  (a) a "2" result on Infantry always eliminates exactly **1** Truck Point (the asterisk's "one" is a
  flat rider, not scaled to Barrage severity), or
  (b) a "2" result eliminates **2** Truck Points (parallel to "As above, except lose two points" scaling
  everything in the "1" definition, including the truck rider). The book prints no explicit "if the
  infantry is in trucks, eliminate two Truck Points" anywhere. I did not find a resolving cross-reference
  in Chapter 12. Recommend (a) — the flat reading — as the more literal one, but this is a call for the
  project owner, not something I resolved by invention.

## Part D — How the two "Trucks" mechanics relate (for the implementer)

There are **three, not two**, distinct rulebook channels that destroy Truck Points, and they must not be
conflated into one:

1. **12.46's secondary roll** — rides along with *every* land Barrage against Infantry/Armor/Gun,
   reading the **Truck row of [12.6]** at the *same* Actual-Barrage-Points column as the primary roll,
   with an independent d66. This is what "12.46" names and what this assignment centers on.
2. **12.46's motorized-Infantry rider** — a *separate*, deterministic truck loss (not a table roll)
   triggered when the *primary* Barrage result lands on Infantry that happens to be riding in Trucks;
   the asterisk footnote under [12.6]'s Infantry "1" result.
3. **[41.5]'s Trucks row** — used when Trucks (or a truck convoy) are themselves the *primary* target of
   an **Air Bombardment mission** (Bomb Points column) or a **rule-12.54 secondary Barrage target**
   (Barrage Points column, footnote b — out of scope here). This is a different rule family (Chapter 41,
   the Air Game) from 12.46 (Chapter 12, the Land Game), and already wired per the commit referenced in
   `scratchpad/p55/commitmsg.txt` ("41.32 B-TC ... Trucks: That number of Truck Points and their cargo
   destroyed").
4. **[41.5]'s Supply Dump row's truck rider** — "In addition, lose 1 Truck Point for each 10%" of
   Supplies destroyed when a Supply Dump (not the Trucks themselves) is the bombed/barraged target. A
   fourth, independent channel, also already wired (per the same commit, `41.35 B-SD`).

Only channel 1 (and its rider, channel 2) is what `game/combat_tables.py` and `game/engine.py` currently
call out as **deferred** under the name "12.46."

## IMPLEMENTATION FLAG — the engine's stated reason for deferring 12.46 misapplies an abstract-game rule

Not a book contradiction (nothing to rule on) but a concrete, load-bearing finding surfaced while
verifying this chart, worth carrying forward to whoever implements 12.46:

- `game/combat_tables.py:259-263`: `_BARRAGE` only has `"infantry"`, `"armor"`, `"gun"` keys — no
  `"truck"` — with the comment *"Trucks (a separate 12.46 roll) are deferred (abstract logistics 32.56
  says barrage never hits trucks)."*
- `game/engine.py:4018` (docstring of `_barrage_step`, re-checked this verification pass — the line moved
  from an earlier `:4012` citation because another workflow is actively editing `game/` in parallel, per
  the orchestrator's own instructions for this session): *"Terrain column-shifts and the separate truck
  roll (12.46) are deferred."*

The cited rule, **32.56**, reads in full (PDF, Chapter 32 "ABSTRACT LOGISTICS AND AIR RULES"):

> [32.56] **Motorization Points** are normally subject to loss only if the unit they are assigned to is
> eliminated. If their assigned unit is captured, the Motorization Points are also captured and may be
> used by the Enemy. **Note that a Barrage Combat Result against Trucks never occurs.**

32.56 is about **Motorization Points**, the Section-32 abstract substitute for Truck Points — 32.51
opens the whole Major Case with "*Motorization Points are used in place of Truck Points*," for players
running the abstract game instead of the full Logistics Game. Per this project's own `CLAUDE.md`:
*"Sections 32, 47 and 58 are the ABSTRACT game and DO NOT APPLY... An abstract-game rule in force is a
bug class, not a shortcut."* This engine runs the full Logistics Game with real Truck Points (54.2), not
Motorization Points — so 32.56's "Barrage never hits Trucks" sentence does not apply here, and citing it
to defer 12.46 is exactly the abstract-game trap CLAUDE.md names. Rule 12.46 (Chapter 12, the full
land-combat game) is unambiguous that Barrage *does* hit real Trucks; nothing in Chapter 12 or 54
contradicts it. This is a transcription/verification doc, not a code change — flagging for whoever picks
up the 12.46 implementation slice, since the current deferral comment would otherwise read as
rule-backed when the rule it cites is the wrong one for this build.
