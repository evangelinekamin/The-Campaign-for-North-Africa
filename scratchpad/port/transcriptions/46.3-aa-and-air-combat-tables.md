## How planes actually get shot down — the three mechanisms, in rulebook order

1. **Air-to-air (rule 45)**: fighter-vs-fighter and fighter-vs-screened-plane combat, resolved plane
   pair by plane pair. TacAir differential (adjusted for pilot rating and the Maneuver Adjustment
   Chart) → look up the **[45.5] TacAir Kill Table** → roll 2d6 sequential, compare to the "Kill:"
   range for that differential.
2. **Anti-aircraft fire / flak (rule 46)**: AA-capable units and Air Facilities fire at planes flying
   missions in their hex, grouped into five Target Groups (46.26). Total AA points → look up the
   **[46.3] Anti-Aircraft Combat Results Table**, using whichever of its three sub-grids matches what
   the target planes are doing (see "The chart is laid out as three adjoining sub-grids" below).
3. **Strafing (rule 40.5–40.8), for GROUNDED aircraft only**: a plane sitting on the ground (not
   flying) can be strafed under the "Grounded Airplanes" result of the **[40.8] Strafing Table** — the
   result there is not a plane count but a *number of dice to roll*, summed for the actual kill count.

Every one of these feeds into the **[45.6] Pilot and Plane Recovery Table** for a chance the plane
(and/or pilot) survives anyway.

## [46.3] ANTI-AIRCRAFT COMBAT RESULTS TABLE

**Location:** PDF p.108 (booklet p.13), rendered at 300/600 dpi (`pdftoppm -r 600`) and cross-read

The chart is laid out as three adjoining sub-grids, all keyed to the same ten AA-point categories, but
each with its own "Result" (planes affected) axis and its own die-roll partition — this is not one
20×10 grid, it is three independently-partitioned grids that happen to share a page:

- **"No. Planes Destroyed"** (Result 0–5) — used for **Planes on Other Missions**.
- **"No. Planes Aborted"** (Result 0–7) — used for **Planes on Other Missions**.

  missions, matching the rule text (fighters aren't described as aborting from flak; the procedure
  only ever calls for a single destroyed-count roll against this sub-grid).

**On-page axis orientation (transcription note, not a rule — PINNED by the adversarial pass):** the
chart is printed sideways on the page (landscape table rotated 90° in a portrait scan). Rendered
upright at 600 dpi, its true layout is: a `Number of Flak Points` header spanning the ten AA-point
**columns** (`1...4 … 37+`) that run left-to-right, a `Result` column at the far right, and each
sub-grid's Result index (0–5 / 0–7 / 0–3) running top-to-bottom as **rows**. So on the page,
**Flak Points is the column axis and Result is the row axis.** The three sub-grids below transpose
this to **AA/Flak Points as rows, Result as columns**, matching the layout of every other CRT in this
port. This is a presentational choice, not a rule; the underlying (AA-points, Result) → dice-range
pairings are unchanged and were verified cell-by-cell against the upright render. (The original
transcription left this orientation "not pinned down with full certainty" and stated it backwards —

## [46.4] FLAK ADJUSTMENT CHART

**Location:** PDF p.106 (booklet p.11). Already in `docs/rules/90-charts-tables-and-play-aids.md`
lines 1436–1443 — **verified against the render, exact match, no discrepancy.**

Referenced by the rule-46 procedure text under the name "AA Density Adjustment Chart" — same chart,
two names (rule prose vs. chart heading).

| Nr. non-Fighter Planes | Columns Adjusted in Favor of Player Firing AA |
|---|---|
| 1 to 23 | 0 |
| 24 to 47 | 1 |
| 48 to 71 | 2 |
| 72 or more | 3 |

Usage per rule 46 procedure text: only applies to **Planes on Other Missions** (non-fighter-type
missions); count total enemy non-fighter planes in the hex, shift the AA-point column right by the
indicated number of columns before consulting Sub-grid A/B above. The rule-46 PROCEDURE prose
(`docs/rules/46-anti-aircraft-fire-flak.md`) calls this the "AA Density Adjustment Chart" and says the
firing player consults it "noting the **total number of enemy planes (other than fighters)** in that
hex."

**OWNER RULING NEEDED — two contradictory column-shift rules for the same step.** The [46.3] chart's
own printed Notes (PDF p.108) and this [46.4] Flak Adjustment Chart (PDF p.106, rule-46 prose) both
tell the firing player how far to shift the Flak-Point column for enemy plane density, and they give
**different shifts for the same plane count**:
- **Reading A — [46.4] chart / rule-46 prose (PDF p.106):** keyed on *total non-fighter planes* in
  bands — `1–23 → 0`, `24–47 → 1`, `48–71 → 2`, `72+ → 3` (i.e. roughly +1 per 24 planes).
- **Reading B — [46.3] chart Notes (PDF p.108):** keyed on *Bomber-Class or Transport-Class aircraft*
  in multiples of twelve — "shift one column for each twelve aircraft exceeding twelve (24 aircraft
  shift one column)" (i.e. +1 per 12 planes: `24 → +1`, `36 → +2`, `48 → +3`).
- **Divergence:** at 36 planes Reading A gives +1, Reading B gives +2; at 48 planes Reading A gives
  +2, Reading B gives +3. They coincide only at 24 planes. This is not a population-scope difference
  (they disagree even for a pure bomber formation) — it is a genuine rate contradiction of the 54.17
  class. **Not resolved here.** The implementer must pick one (or apply both, which the prose does not
  license) under an owner ruling.

|---|---|---|---|
| Strafed | R | R | L |

## Rule text this doc leans on (for the implementer, not re-transcribed as charts)

- **46.0 procedure** (`docs/rules/46-anti-aircraft-fire-flak.md` lines 8–24): Target Groups are formed
  per 46.26 (five kinds: daytime non-fighter-type by mission, night non-fighter-type by mission,
  daytime fighter-type CAP/scramble/interception, night fighter-type CAP/scramble/interception,
  fighter-type strafing/dive-bombing). "Do not divide AA points by ten" (unlike other unit strengths).
  Fighter-type target groups → sub-grid C, single roll for destroyed count. Other-missions target
  groups → [46.4]-adjust the AA-point column first, then roll **twice** against sub-grids A and B (once
  for destroyed via sub-grid A, once for aborted via sub-grid B), destroyed determined before aborted.
- **45.0 procedure**: TacAir differential = own (TacAir + pilot rating [+ formation bonus for bombers])
  − enemy's same, then adjusted by [45.4] in favor of the higher-Maneuver plane. Lower/negative
  differential fires first (ties broken by higher pilot rating, then attacker). [45.5] gives the kill
  threshold for a sequential 2d6 roll.
- **40.0/40.5–40.8**: strafing is TacAir-points-based (not AA-points), targets grounded aircraft
  among other target types, and the "Grounded Airplanes" row of [40.8] is a dice-count not a kill-count
  (40.63).
