Reading notes on the table itself, as printed:
- A blank/inherited cell ("*(same)*" above) means the row is literally shaded through with **no
  distinct value printed** — Village/Bir/Oasis and Railroad inherit every column, including Stacking
  Limit, from the hex's underlying terrain. This is *not* a dash; a dash ("–") is a distinct printed
  glyph meaning "no effect / not applicable" and appears only on hexside rows, fortification-level
  rows, and the minefield rows' Stacking column.
- A dash in the **Stacking Limit** column (all hexside rows, the Fortification-level rows, both
  Minefield rows) means these are not independent hex-occupancy types — a Ridge, an Up Slope, a Level
  Two Fortification, a Minefield, etc. are all *overlays* on a hex whose own terrain (Clear, Rough,
  Major City, …) already supplies the Stacking Limit. There is no second, competing number.
- "Major City … *See Fortifications*" for all three combat columns: a Major City's Barrage/Anti-Armor/
  Close-Assault terrain adjustment is not fixed — it comes from whatever Fortification Level (0–3) the
  city hex actually carries (rule 25.11/25.22), read off the Fortifications rows below, with note 12
  gating when armor gets the Anti-Armor/Barrage version of that benefit.

### Notes (verbatim)

> 1. A garrison unit in its assigned hex occupies zero stacking space. In addition, an unlimited
>    number of anti-aircraft units may occupy a Major City hex at zero stacking space; three
>    anti-aircraft units may occupy an Airfield hex at zero stacking space while one anti-aircraft
>    unit may occupy an Air Landing Strip hex at zero stacking space.
> 2. Vehicles *other than* Light Trucks, Recce-type units, and motorcycle infantry may only
>    enter/leave the hex on a track. Motorized units may not engage in combat into/out of a Salt
>    Marsh (Case 8.44).
> 3. Light Trucks, motorcycle reconnaissance units, and motorcycle infantry units may not enter,
>    even via a track.
> 4. Alexandria and Cairo hexes are Level Three Fortifications, all others are Level Two.
> 5. Commonwealth units may be able to use Rail Movement (Case 8.7).
> 6. Negates all hexside terrain feature entry costs and Breakdown Point Values.
> 7. Applies only to the units/truck points in that hex at that time.
> 8. Halves all terrain feature entry costs and all hex/hexside Breakdown Point values *except* the
>    Capability Points expended and the Breakdown acquired by moving a vehicle down an escarpment.
> 9. Motorized units and trucks may only cross on a track.
> 10. May only be crossed during a Rainstorm if the hexside is crossed by a road or railroad (which is
>     treated as a road for this purpose) at a cost of two additional Capability Points.
> 11. May only be crossed if the hexside is crossed by a road or railroad. A motorized unit may so
>     cross at no additional cost in Capability or Breakdown Points.
> 12. Armor targets receive the defensive benefit only if the hex is a Major City hex.
> 13. Engineer units reduce cost to enter (Case 26.2). If *assaulting* forces are in an Enemy
>     minefield, the non-Phasing forces receive L1 shifts for Anti-Armor and Close Assault if not
>     already receiving them for occupying a Friendly minefield.

### Chart oddity worth flagging (not a rules contradiction, just noted)

The superscript "⁴" is printed next to **Swamp** in the Terrain Type column (`Swamp⁴`), but footnote
4's text is entirely about Major City fortification levels (Alexandria/Cairo = Level 3, all other
Major Cities = Level 2) and has no evident connection to Swamp. This is almost certainly a typesetting
placement artifact in the original chart, not a real Swamp rule: footnote 4's content matches rule
[25.12] near-verbatim ("Each major city on the game-map is a Level 2 fortification. Cairo and
Alexandria are Level 3 Fortifications"), which `game/scenario.py`'s `_DELTA_FORT = 3` /
`delta_hexes()` already encodes correctly, citing 25.12 directly. Not an "OWNER RULING NEEDED" — the
book doesn't print two different values for the same fact, it just mis-set the footnote marker's row.
Zoomed crop confirming the "⁴" sits on "Swamp" (not "Major City") saved as
`crop_majorcity_swamp.png` in this session's scratchpad.

### 2. Stacking Limit — CONFIRMED GAP, this is the chart's deliverable for this transcription

`game/stacking.py` lines 9–22 already states the honest situation: *"the per-terrain hex limits live on
the 8.37 chart / counter sheets (§9.4 'separate sheet') and did NOT survive OCR (the OCR'd 8.37 has no
stacking column). So the hex limit is injected by the caller; `DEFAULT_HEX_LIMIT` below is a
placeholder."* That placeholder is `5`. The real, scan-verified per-terrain values (rule 9.14: "the
maximum number of Stacking Points which may be in that hex at the end of any Movement Segment") are:

```
HEX_STACKING_LIMIT = {
    "clear": 6, "gravel": 6, "salt_marsh": 6, "heavy_veg": 6, "rough": 6,
    "mountain": 3, "delta": 6, "desert": 6, "major_city": 8,
    "swamp": 6,   # terrain type not yet in game/terrain.py's Terrain enum -- see §4
    # village/bir/oasis, railroad: NOT independent values -- inherit the underlying
    # terrain's limit (the chart prints these rows fully blank, not dashed)
}
```

Notes for whoever wires this in:
- Every terrain type is **6** except **Mountain = 3** and **Major City = 8**. The current placeholder
  `DEFAULT_HEX_LIMIT = 5` is not equal to any real value on the chart — it happens to sit between the
  common case (6) and the tightest case (3), which is presumably why nothing has obviously broken, but
  it is wrong for every single terrain type.
- Hexside features (Ridge, slopes, escarpments, rivers), the three Fortification levels, and both
  Minefield rows all print a dash in this column — they are not separate hex-occupancy types, they
  modify a hex whose *terrain* row already supplies the limit (§ table reading notes above). Any
  implementation should NOT try to look up a stacking limit keyed on hexside features or fortification
  level; it should key on the hex's base `Terrain`.
- `stacking.ROAD_TRACK_STACK_LIMIT = 5` (rule 9.33, "5-point limit for road/track movement") is a
  **separate, already-correct** number — the chart's Road/Track rows independently confirm it: both
  print **"5⁷"** in the Stacking Limit column, footnote 7 ("Applies only to the units/truck points in
  that hex at that time") being exactly the rule-9.33 movement-through-stack case, distinct from the
  rule-9.14 end-of-Movement-Segment hex cap the terrain-keyed table above governs. Do not conflate the
  two: a Clear hex caps at 6 for who's *sitting* there at Segment end, but a unit road/track-moving
  *through* a stack in that same hex is limited to 5 points of the units/trucks already present at
  that moment.
- `tests/test_reinforcements.py` builds "hex full" fixtures off `stacking.DEFAULT_HEX_LIMIT` (lines 72,
  82) — once the placeholder is replaced by a per-terrain table, those fixtures will need to source
  their hex's actual terrain-keyed limit (6 for whatever default terrain the fixture uses, not 5) to
  keep "hex full" meaning what it says. Flagging for the implementing phase; not touched here
  (read-only on `tests/`).
