## 20.8 Commonwealth MANDATORY Withdrawals — the rule

Historical basis: Commonwealth units were repeatedly pulled from the desert for Crete, Greece,
Syria, Iraq, Cyprus, Sudan, etc. The Reinforcement Schedule ([4.43a]) tells the CW Player **if,
when, and how many** units must be removed.

- **[20.81]** Withdrawals are listed on the Reinforcement Schedule, either as **named units**
  ("WD: 5th In Bde") or **by type** ("Withdraw any two Armor Brigades"). In [4.43a] almost every
  WD is a named unit; the only by-type entry is GT97 ("...and any three infantry battalions").
- **[20.82]** A by-type withdrawal must be satisfied with a unit at **≥75% max TOE Strength**;
  if none is that strong, the **highest-TOE unit of that type** available.
- **[20.83]** Units to be withdrawn **must be moved to Cairo or Alexandria on or before** the
  GT/Stage of withdrawal. **If not present (or not at required TOE) by then, they are ELIMINATED**
  — an anti-procrastination mechanic. **Units so eliminated may NOT return** (whereas many normal
  withdrawals do return later). Note: 20.83 cites "(20.75)" for the required TOE — that is a
  misprint (see OWNER RULING below).
- **[20.84]** Once at Cairo/Alexandria, the unit is simply **removed from play**.
- **[20.85]** A unit below 75% TOE scheduled for withdrawal must be brought up to 75% by
  Replacement Points **before** the withdrawal date, **or** an equivalent ≥75% substitute of
  similar type is withdrawn in its place.

**Trigger:** the schedule, not player choice. **Return:** per the schedule's separate `Rtn:`
rows (a returning unit arrives fresh at max TOE at Cairo/Alexandria, auto-upgraded, tank/gun
units may be refitted). **Victory tie:** NONE — mandatory (Reinforcement-Track) withdrawals earn
**no** Victory Points (see 64.75).

**Which battalions / when (summary):** the withdrawals fall heaviest on the veteran colonial
formations that history sent elsewhere — the **4th Indian Div** (GT13/14/15, returns and is
re-withdrawn 36/75/100), the **6th & 9th Australian Divs** (GT26–28, 46, 49, 53–54, 108), the
**2nd New Zealand Div** (GT25, re-withdrawn 72 — sent to Greece/Crete then Syria), the **70th

(GT13–28) strips the desert army repeatedly; the late withdrawals (GT88–110) pull armor (7 Armd
Bde HQ, 25 Bde Group tanks, 10 Armd Div HQ, 9 Armd Bde) as those formations were re-roled.

## 20.9 VOLUNTARY Commonwealth Withdrawals — the rule

Beyond the large mandatory withdrawals, the CW historically sent **ad hoc battalions** to Cyprus,
Sudan, Iraq, etc., shuffling them back and forth almost at random. The book declines to schedule
these ("It would be folly to list all such movements") and instead makes them a **player option**:

- The CW Player **may withdraw small forces at any time**, thereby **gaining Victory Points**
  (these forces notionally aid campaigns elsewhere / pressure the enemy).
- **Returning** a voluntarily-withdrawn unit **penalizes** the CW Player.
- Governed entirely by the Campaign Game Victory Conditions → **64.75**.

The designer flags the mechanism as reluctant ("We are not too happy with Mandatory Withdrawals,
but such unit absences on a large scale must be accounted for").

## The victory tie — 64.75 (Withdrawal Points, Commonwealth only)

[64.75] applies **solely to the Commonwealth**, to battalions withdrawn **other than via the
Reinforcement Track** — i.e. **voluntary** (20.9) withdrawals ONLY. **Mandatory (20.8) withdrawals
score nothing.**

- **A.** For every **combat battalion** (not company) or battalion-equivalent of **infantry,
  armour, artillery or anti-tank guns (NOT AA)** that is **≥75% TOE Strength** and is withdrawn,
  the CW receives **½ point per week gone, max 3 points per unit** (so ≥6 weeks caps it). To be
  withdrawn a unit **must start the Stage in Alexandria or Cairo**.
- **B.** Each time an A-withdrawn battalion is **returned**, the CW **loses 2 points**. A unit
  voluntarily withdrawn then returned **may not be withdrawn again for six months** from the
  return date (kills the "One-Point Two Step" of shuffling one unit in and out).

(64.74, the line above, is unused-Replacement-Point VPs — a different clause, listed here only to
mark the boundary.)

## Data / code cross-check — what is seeded vs. missing

`grep -ri withdraw data/` → the only hit is `data/air_reinforcements_34_86.json` (the **air**
squadron-withdrawal column, 34.85/34.86 — transcribed-but-**unapplied**). The **land** withdrawals
of [4.43a] are **NOT seeded as removals** anywhere.

- `data/reinforcements_campaign.json` (541 rows) seeds the **`(Rtn)` returning units as fresh
  arrivals** (47 rows tagged "(Rtn)", e.g. "4 In Div core (Rtn)" arrival_turn 28, "HQ 16 Inf Bde
  (Rtn)" arrival_turn 51). It has **no `WD` / withdrawal / removal field** — units are never
  removed at their WD turn.
- Consequence: today the campaign **adds** the returning formations back but **never subtracts**
  the withdrawn ones. The desert army only grows; the historical stripping (Crete/Greece/Syria)
  does not happen, and the 20.83 "eliminated for procrastination" branch cannot fire.
- `game/campaign_victory.py:33` **explicitly DEFERS** "64.74 unused-Replacement-Point VPs and
  **64.75 Commonwealth Withdrawal VPs**." So voluntary withdrawals score nothing yet either.

**Net:** 20.8/20.9 are effectively unimplemented. Returns are modelled (as arrivals); the
withdrawals themselves, their Tpt-escort requirement, the 20.83 elimination mechanic, and the
64.75 voluntary-withdrawal VP loop are all absent. A faithful implementation needs a **removal
event keyed to the [4.43a] WD rows above** (with the Cairo/Alexandria-presence gate and the
eliminate-if-absent rule), plus wiring 64.75 to the voluntary-withdrawal path.

## OWNER RULINGS NEEDED

1. **20.83 cross-reference misprint (scan p. 31 / docs/rules/20 line 138).** 20.83 says a unit not
   present "or at required TOE Strength **(20.75)**" is eliminated. **20.75 is the wrong case** — it
   is about CW Production Replacement Points arriving in addition to Production, nothing to do with
   TOE. The 75%-TOE requirement lives in **20.82** (and 20.85 correctly cites 20.82). The reference
   is near-certainly a typo for **20.82**. Recommend reading it as 20.82; flag rather than silently
   "correct" per port rule 1.

2. **"per week" granularity for 64.75-A.** 64.75-A pays "½ point for **each week** that unit is
   gone." CNA Game-Turns are weekly (111 turns ≈ Sept 1940→Dec 1942), so "week" = **one Game-Turn**
   (not OpStage). Cheap assumption, but confirm before wiring: 3-OpStage turns make "week" ambiguous
   on its face.

3. **GT numbering of [4.43a] is by the Reinforcement-Track GT index, not calendar.** The WD turns
   above are the chart's GT column (1–110); when wiring, map to the same GT axis the campaign
   reinforcement data already uses (`arrival_turn`), which the `(Rtn)` rows confirm is that index.
