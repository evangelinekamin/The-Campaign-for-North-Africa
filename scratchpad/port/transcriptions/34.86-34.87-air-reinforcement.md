## The book's own chart-numbering mismatch (extends an already-recorded finding)

**`data/air_reinforcements_34_86.json`'s `_chart_label` already documents this for the Commonwealth
chart** — I independently re-derived the same finding while rendering page 177 for the Axis chart, so
it is restated here in full because it is the same defect on the sibling chart:

The rule prose (34.85–34.89, `docs/rules/34-the-aircraft.md`) reads:

> [34.85] The Commonwealth Player is required to withdraw certain squadrons... The dates, squadron
> types and plane requirements are listed in the Commonwealth Airplane Reinforcement and Squadron
> Withdrawal Schedule (**Case 34.86**).
>
> [34.86] Commonwealth Airplane Reinforcement and Squadron Withdrawal Schedule (see Charts and Tables)
>
> [34.87] Axis Airplane Reinforcement Schedule (see Charts and Tables)
>
> [34.88] Commonwealth Pilot Arrival Table. (see Charts and Tables)
>
> [34.89] Axis Pilot Arrival Tables. (see Charts and Tables)

But the charts themselves, read with eyes off the scan, print:

| What the rule-prose calls it | What is actually printed on the chart (PDF page) |
|---|---|
| 34.86 Commonwealth Airplane Reinforcement/Withdrawal Schedule | **`[34.84]` COMMONWEALTH AIRPLANE REINFORCEMENT AND SQUADRON WITHDRAWAL CHART** (p.143) |
| 34.87 Axis Airplane Reinforcement Schedule | **`[34.85]` AXIS AIRPLANE REINFORCEMENT CHART** (p.177) |
| 34.88 Commonwealth Pilot Arrival Table | **`[34.86]` COMMONWEALTH PILOT ARRIVAL TABLE** (p.142) |
| 34.89 (a/b) Axis Pilot Arrival Tables | **`[34.89a]`/`[34.89b]` ITALIAN/GERMAN PILOT ARRIVAL TABLE** (p.178) — this one *does* match the rule text |

Every chart in the block is numbered **two lower** than the case that names it, except the last
(Pilot Arrival Tables), which is correct. Reading `34-the-aircraft.md`'s prose, 34.84 is the case that
*describes how airplane reinforcements arrive* (the general mechanism, no chart of its own) —
consistent with the printer having carried case 34.84's number down onto the two airplane charts that
immediately follow it in the layout, and having similarly mis-set 34.86's number onto the pilot table
that follows *that*. This is a **printer/layout slip in the original 1979 book**, not a content
dispute — the chart titles ("...AIRPLANE REINFORCEMENT AND SQUADRON WITHDRAWAL CHART",
"AXIS AIRPLANE REINFORCEMENT CHART") are unambiguous about which schedule each one is, independent of
the bracketed number printed over them. **This document (and `data/air_reinforcements_34_86.json`)
refer to the charts by the case that names them in rule prose (34.86 Commonwealth, 34.87 Axis),
recording the printed-but-wrong numbers here rather than propagating them.**

## Rule prose, verbatim (PDF p.061, `docs/rules/34-the-aircraft.md` lines 100–148)

> **34.8 AIRFORCE REINFORCEMENTS AND WITHDRAWLS** — The Players receive airforce reinforcements in
> the forms of individual pilots, individual planes and Squadron Ground Support Units (SGSU's). Low
> ranked pilots arrive as needed while higher ranked pilots arrive randomly. SGSU's arrive as needed.
> **Airplane reinforcements follow a fixed schedule**; in addition, the Commonwealth Player is
> required to remove various squadrons during the course of the game.
>
> **[34.81]** The Commonwealth Player's airforce reinforcements may be placed in any air facility in
> Malta, Cairo, Alexandria, or Off-map except Ethiopia. The reinforcements may be divided amongst the
> receiving air facilities in any distribution the Commonwealth Player chooses except as follows: A/
> No more than 10% of a month's airplane reinforcements may be sent to Malta. B/ No airplane
> reinforcements may be sent to a Malta/N African Off-map air facility in excess of the facility's
> current squadron capacity. **The Axis Player's airforce reinforcements may be divided amongst the
> bases in the Tripoli/Tunisia boxes and Italy/Sicily/Crete as he chooses** within the requirements
> concerning basing German bombers in the Mediterranean (see Case 43.1).
>
> **[34.84]** Airplane reinforcements arrive in the Naval Convoy Arrival Phase. The reinforcements are
> listed as to the total of each type of plane arriving during that month (the beginning of the
> campaign has arrivals during certain specific gameturns instead of months). The planes must be
> divided amongst the weeks as evenly as possible and all of the planes arriving during a game-turn
> may arrive in the First Op Stage of that game-turn at the owning Player's choice (i.e., the Players
> need only divide the total by four, not twelve).
>
> **[34.85]** The Commonwealth Player is required to withdraw certain squadrons during the course of
> the game. The dates, squadron types and plane requirements are listed in the Commonwealth Airplane
> Reinforcement and Squadron Withdrawal Schedule (Case 34.86). Squadron withdrawals are performed at
> the beginning of the month indicated. Squadrons may be withdrawn from any air facility on Malta or
> game map section "E". The Commonwealth Player simply removes from play all of the planes and pilots
> assigned to that squadron. SGSU's are not removed unless they now exceed the limit determined in
> Case 34.82.

Note what this prose does **not** say: there is no parallel Axis squadron-withdrawal rule anywhere in
34.8. The Axis chart (Part B below) carries **no WD column at all** — the Axis Player receives
aeroplanes on a fixed schedule and never has to remove any. This is a real asymmetry, not a
transcription gap: I read all three columns of PDF p.177 end to end and no withdrawal notation of any
kind appears on the Axis chart, and no rule case anywhere in 34.8 imposes one on the Axis. (Contrast
this with what is separately levied against the Axis air force by Chapter 43's Mediterranean-basing
requirements, which is a different mechanism — moving planes off the African mainland, not removing
them from play.)

### Flags — read carefully before wiring this chart

1. **UNREADABLE CELL, FLAGGED RATHER THAN GUESSED — January 1942 Italian BR20 quantity.** The chart
   prints (verified at 4× zoom, crop `jan42_brline_4x.png`): `"B × BR20"` — a **literal capital letter
   "B"** where every other cell in this 28-row chart has a numeral. This is not an OCR artifact on my
   part or on `docs/rules/90-charts-tables-and-play-aids.md`'s part — the existing OCR transcription
   (line 5810) independently reads the same `"B×BR20"`, and my own fresh 600 dpi render at 4× zoom
   confirms the glyph is shaped as a serif "B" (two stacked closed bowls off a flat left stroke), not
   an "8" (compare the unambiguous "8"s two words earlier in the same line, "**18**×Re2001" — visibly
   different letterform). **Two independent transcriptions agree the book prints something that does
   not parse as a number.** The most plausible reading is a **misprint for "8"** (visual confusion
   between a bold "B" and a bold "8" in 1970s offset print is a known failure mode, and 8×BR20 keeps
   the row's Italian total at a sane ~90, next to Feb 1942's 90 and Dec 1941's 192) — but I did not
   find a way to *confirm* 8 over any other single digit from the page itself, and this project's rule
   is transcribe-don't-invent. **OWNER RULING NEEDED: what quantity of BR20 (Fiat B.R.20 Cicogna
   bomber) does the Axis Player receive in January 1942 (GT63–66)?** Recommend reading it as 8 (visual
   nearest-neighbor, keeps the row internally consistent with its neighbors) but flagging rather than
   silently assuming.

2. **BOOK-INTERNAL INCONSISTENCY — the `*`/`†` footnotes name S.M.79, but in TWO 1942 rows the mark is
   printed against S.M.84 instead.** (Corrected by adversarial re-render — the original draft of this
   document reported only the May-1942 case and wrongly called it "the only row"; a fresh cell-by-cell
   read of PDF p.177 at 600 dpi found a **second** instance one row earlier that the first pass had
   dropped. Both are recorded below.)
   - **April 1942 (GT75–78):** the Italian clause ends `"...12×SM79, 12†×SM84"` — a **dagger (†) on the
     12×SM84 quantity** (verified at high zoom, crop `apr42_sm84b.png`; the first draft omitted this
     dagger entirely). The `†` footnote reads "**Twelve** of these S.M.79's were operated as
     torpedo-bombers" and this SM84 quantity is exactly 12 — the number matches the footnote, only the
     aircraft type it is attached to does not.
   - **May 1942 (GT79–82):** the Italian clause ends `"...6×SM79, 6*×SM84"` — an **asterisk (*) on the
     6×SM84 quantity** (verified at high zoom, crop `may42_sm84c.png`). The `*` footnote reads "**Six**
     of these S.M.79's were operated as torpedo bombers" and this SM84 quantity is exactly 6 — again the
     number matches, the type does not.

   Every OTHER use of `*`/`†` on the chart attaches to an S.M.79 quantity, matching the Key's wording
   (`*` on Jun41 24×SM79, Aug41 18×SM79, Mar42 6×SM79; `†` on Nov41 36×SM79, Sep42 12×SM79). So the
   attachment-to-SM84 is NOT a one-off — it happens in **both** consecutive Apr/May-1942 rows, and in
   both the marked quantity equals the footnote's stated count. This is the 54.17 class of book
   self-contradiction (a footnote and its own attachment point disagree), **recorded rather than
   silently moved.** Two readings: (a) the Key's "S.M.79's" wording is generic (standing in for the
   month's designated torpedo type) and the marks correctly identify torpedo-armed S.M.84s — historically
   the S.M.84 replaced the S.M.79 as Italy's front-line torpedo bomber through 1942, so two adjacent
   1942 rows both marking S.M.84 is exactly what one would expect, and S.M.84 does carry a bomb/torpedo
   rating on `[4.44b]` (`"S.M. 84 | 101 | (4) | 16 | 22/T14 | 4"`, per the OCR read this session); or
   (b) both asterisk/dagger placements are typesetting slips and the marks were meant for that row's
   S.M.79 quantity. The two-rows-in-a-row pattern (and the quantity-matches-footnote fact) makes reading
   (a) the more likely of the two, but this is **OWNER RULING NEEDED**, flagged rather than resolved; it
   does not change any plane count either way, only which type's torpedo-fraction bookkeeping (relevant
   only if `data/air_reinforcements_34_86.json`'s `torpedo_types_4_44a` pattern is extended to the Axis
   side).

3. **Cosmetic scan/print artifact, not a genuine ambiguity — "GF" for "GT" in Aug/Sep 1942.** Both the
   August 1942 and September 1942 month headers print `"(GF 91...94)"` / `"(GF 95...98)"` rather than
   the "(GT ...)" pattern every other one of the chart's 26 other month headers uses (verified at 4×
   zoom, crop `aug42_gf_4x.png` — the glyph is a clean serif "F", not a damaged "T"). Both the existing
   OCR (independently) and my fresh render agree on "GF", so this is very likely a genuine defect in
   the 1979 printing plate (the same slug reused for two consecutive months would explain the repeat)
   rather than a scan/OCR error — but it carries no ambiguity of *meaning*: "Game-Turn" is what every
   other instance of the abbreviation means throughout the entire book, there is no other defined
   sense of "GF" anywhere in Chapter 34 or the Glossary, and the numeric ranges (91...94, 95...98)
   fall exactly where GT91–94 and GT95–98 belong in the unbroken monthly GT sequence this chart runs
   start to finish. Read as **GT** in the table above; noted here rather than silently normalized.

4. **Inconsistent capitalization, not a type ambiguity — "Ju88c" vs "Ju88C".** The Ju.88C appears six
   times across the chart; four are printed `"Ju88C"` (capital C — Nov41, Jan42, Apr42, Jul42) and two
   are printed `"Ju88c"` (lowercase — Feb42, Aug42). Both forms are read as the same aircraft (the
   Ju.88C heavy fighter/night-fighter, distinct from the Ju.88D reconnaissance/bomber variant, which
   the chart always capitalizes consistently). This is ordinary 1970s phototypesetting inconsistency,
   not a book contradiction — normalized to `Ju88C` in the table above.

5. **No withdrawal column, confirmed by omission, not oversight.** See "Rule prose, verbatim" above —
   this is a real rule asymmetry (only the Commonwealth is required to withdraw squadrons; the Axis is
   not), not a transcription gap on this chart.

6. **BOOK-INTERNAL NAMING INCONSISTENCY — the Cant Z.1007 is printed two ways: `Z1007` in 1941, `Z1007bis`
   in 1942.** (Corrected by adversarial re-render — the first draft silently normalized the two 1941
   rows to "Z1007bis"; the scan prints only "Z1007" there.) Verified cell-by-cell at 600 dpi:
   - **`Z1007`** (no `bis` suffix) is printed in **Feb 1941** (`6×Z1007`, crop `c1_feb_mar.png`) and
     **Mar 1941** (`6×Z1007`, same crop) — restored to `Z1007` in the table above.
   - **`Z1007bis`** (with `bis`) is printed in **May 1942** (`12×Z1007bis`), **Jun 1942**
     (`12×Z1007bis`), **Jul 1942** (`12×Z1007bis`) and **Sep 1942** (`12×Z1007bis`, crop `c4b.png`) —
     these are printed with the suffix and are transcribed verbatim as such.

## Appendix — Pilot Arrival Tables (34.88/34.89), out of primary scope, spot-verified in passing

Not part of this assignment (34.86/34.87 cover **aeroplanes**; 34.88/34.89 cover **pilots**, a
separate reinforcement channel per 34.83) and **not transcribed into `data/` by this document** — but
since the scan pages carrying them (142, 178) were open anyway, I cross-checked all three tables
(Commonwealth `[34.86]`-printed/34.88-actual on p.142; Italian `[34.89a]` and German `[34.89b]` on
p.178) cell-by-cell against the existing OCR in `docs/rules/90-charts-tables-and-play-aids.md` lines
3511–3529 and 5962–5999. **All three tables match the OCR exactly, zero discrepancies** — no chart
this pass touched needed correction the way the aeroplane charts occasionally did. Two details worth
flagging for whoever picks up 34.88/34.89 as their own assignment:

- **The German Pilot Arrival Table has a different active date range than the Italian and Commonwealth
  tables.** Commonwealth (p.142) and Italian (p.178) both read *"only rolls/uses this table once a
  month for the months of November, 1940 through October, 1942 inclusive."* The German table (p.178)
  reads instead: *"The Axis Player only uses this table once a month during **March, 1941 through
  October, 1942** inclusive."* — four months later than its Italian counterpart. This tracks the same
  history the 34.87 chart above encodes (no German air presence in Africa before February 1941).
- **Hauptmann Hans-Joachim Marseille's automatic arrival is dated on the chart, not just in rule
  prose.** Rule 34.83 only says he "arrives automatically." The German Pilot Arrival Table itself
  (p.178) is more specific: *"In addition: In any Operation Stage of Game-Turn 29, the German Air
  Force receives a pilot with a Rating of Six."* GT29 is the fourth week of April 1941 — worth carrying
  forward as the actual trigger condition rather than "automatically" with no turn number, if/when
  34.88/34.89 get their own transcription pass.
