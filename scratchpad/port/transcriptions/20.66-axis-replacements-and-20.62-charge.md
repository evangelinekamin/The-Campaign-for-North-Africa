## 1. Where this sits in the rule (verbatim, PDF p.032)

### 20.6 AXIS PLANNED REPLACEMENTS

> Italian and German Replacement Points enter the game from the Axis Replacement Pool
> (20.66) as a result of direct planning by the Axis Player.

**[20.61]** In the Axis Replacement Pool, the Axis Player will find the maximum number of
Replacement Points for each type of point for the entire Campaign Game. (Each Scenario
lists the maximum available for that Scenario, if any). The Axis Player may never use more
Replacement Points than are provided. This is further limited by the maximum points
available per turn, per type.

**[20.62]** The Axis Player may bring in any number of Replacement Points of any and all
Classes during any one OpStage (see Case 20.23) limited of course by the pool
restrictions. However, each Replacement Point is counted against the Shipping Tonnage
allowance for that Game-Turn (see Case 51.5). The equivalent tonnage of each Point is
given in the Pool Table. Thus, if the Axis Player wishes to bring in 10 Italian Infantry
Replacement Points, he would need 350 Tons of Shipping availability.

**[20.63]** All Axis Replacement Points coming from the Axis Replacement Pool must be
Planned for at least two turns in advance. In other words, Axis Planned Replacements for
July, 1941 must be determined in the Naval Convoy Arrival Phase of the 1/III Game-Turn of
June, 1941. Replacement planned in June III may arrive in any OP Stage Naval Convoy
Arrival Phase of July I.

**[20.64]** Replacement Points have priority in Shipping Space over any type of supplies.
The Axis Logistics Commander thus has to keep a close watch (and tight rein) on what he is
shipping at what time, as he rarely knows that far in advance exactly how much shipping he
will have available (see Section 51.0). Remember, Axis Shipping is subject to Commonwealth
air Raid, so keep an eye on Malta and the location of Commonwealth airfields.

**[20.65]** Replacement Points may not be absorbed by units that have no place for them, so
remember to space your Replacements carefully.

**[20.67]** Axis Replacement Point Type Limitations Chart (see Charts and Tables)

### 20.7 COMMONWEALTH PRODUCTION

**[20.71]** Most Commonwealth units and some of their Replacements arrive via the
Reinforcement Track. However, most Replacements — mostly "drafts" and transport — arrive
somewhat randomly as mandated by the Commonwealth Production Tables (20.78).

**[20.72]** The Commonwealth Player must plan two months in advance (unless specified in a
given Scenario) for the arrival of Replacement Points via Production. Thus, for Points
required for August, 1942, the Commonwealth Player must plan — using the Production
Systems — in the first Naval Convoy Schedule Phase of July, 1942. However, the Commonwealth
Player always uses the Production Table for the month (one month in the future) in which he
wishes his units to arrive — not the month in which he is consulting it.

**[20.73]** The Commonwealth Production System functions like the Axis Pools except for
Infantry and Truck Points. The Player uses a random dice roll to determine what Infantry
and Truck Points he receives per turn, depending upon the date of arrival.

**[20.74]** Replacement Points scheduled for any given turn must be divided among the
OpStages as evenly as possible. This refers to the total Points for all types. Thus the
Commonwealth Player may bring 10 Medium Trucks in one OpStage and 10 Infantry Points in the
next.

**[20.75]** Replacement Points listed in the Reinforcement Schedule arrive in addition to
any Production. **The Commonwealth Player has no Shipping Problems; his Replacement Points
simply arrive.**

**[20.76]** All Planned Replacements arrive in any hex in Cairo or Alexandria. They, like
other Replacements, must undergo Training (see Case 20.43).

**[20.77]** The Commonwealth Player may "upgrade" his Infantry units in much the same
fashion as his Recce/AC units. [...] (full text out of scope here — see rule 20.5 /
20.51–20.54 for the Axis-side mirror of this mechanic, Upgrading Armored Car
Effectiveness, both verified on the same p.032 render, unchanged from the existing OCR.)

**[20.78]** Commonwealth Production System (see Charts and Tables) — **out of scope for

## 2. The asymmetry this task asked to capture

The book states it twice, in two chapters, and the two statements agree with each other
(no contradiction on this point — the contradiction found below is about the *tonnage
rate*, not the existence of the asymmetry):

> **[20.62]** "...each Replacement Point is counted against the **Shipping Tonnage**
> allowance for that Game-Turn... The equivalent tonnage of each Point is given in the Pool
> Table."
> **[20.64]** "Replacement Points have **priority in Shipping Space over any type of
> supplies.**" (i.e. over fuel, ammo, stores, water — all of it, not just fuel specifically;
> the task brief's "priority over fuel" is the sharpest instance of a wider priority over
> *all* commodities)
> **[20.63]** Must be Planned **two Game-Turns** in advance.
>
> ...versus...
>
> **[20.75]** "**The Commonwealth Player has no Shipping Problems; his Replacement Points
> simply arrive.**"
> **[20.72]** CW instead plans **two months** (= four Game-Turns, at 2 turns/month) in
> advance — a longer lead time, but with zero tonnage cost and zero competition against its
> own fuel/ammo/stores convoys, because CW replacements are not shipped by convoy at all —
> they simply appear at Cairo/Alexandria (20.76).

So the asymmetry is not just "Axis pays, CW doesn't" — it is structural: Axis RPs are a
*convoy cargo* that must out-compete fuel/ammo/stores for the same finite monthly tonnage
roll (Chapter 56, the Axis Naval Convoy System) and can be sunk by Commonwealth air/naval
interdiction of that convoy; Commonwealth RPs are **not modeled as cargo at all** — they
have no shipping representation, no interdiction exposure, nothing. This is exactly the
"historical engine of the campaign" callout already in
`scratchpad/port/12-20-combat-and-reinforcements.md:224-227`, now with the precise rule
text and pages behind it.

**Downstream chart hook:** `[54.5]` EQUIVALENT WEIGHTS CHART (`90-charts:1533–1561`, PDF
p.110) lists, under "One Replacement Point By":

| One Replacement Point By | Equivalent Tonnage |
| --- | --- |
| Axis Naval Convoy | Varies, see Axis Replacement Pool |
| Interport or Railroad | na (see below) |
| Air | 2* |

i.e. 54.5 explicitly **punts the Axis-convoy rate back to this 20.66 chart** — 54.5 is not
an independent source of the per-point tonnage, it is a forward-reference. The footnote key
(`90-charts:1561`) reads: `† = Note that only infantry-class replacement points may be
transported by air.` So an Axis Infantry Replacement Point can alternatively go by air at a
flat 2 tons/point (no other class may fly). This is a real, distinct third shipping mode for
Axis Infantry RPs specifically that a later implementation phase should keep in mind, even
though it isn't part of the primary Pool Table below.

**Cross-checked the worked example's arithmetic against the Dates column (closing a
potential off-by-2-Game-Turn trap):** the Key's own worked example says the Axis Player
"may only bring in up to 15 of those [Light AA] points during **Game-Turns 4 thru 26**,"
but the Light AA row's own **Dates** cell reads **"2...24"**, not "4...26" — read in
isolation these look like two different windows. They are not: per the Key's own
definition, the **Dates** column is the *planning* window ("The first game-turn on which
the item may be planned... and therefore arrives two game-turns later"), so the *arrival*
window is the printed Dates range shifted **+2 Game-Turns** at both ends: planning
2...24 → arrival (2+2)...(24+2) = **4...26**, exactly matching the worked example. Verified
this reconciles cleanly for Light AA specifically (I did the arithmetic against the
zoomed Dates cell, not just the OCR). **Implementation trap:** every Dates/Date cell in
both the German and Italian charts is a *planning* Game-Turn, not an *arrival* Game-Turn —
a naive port that keys arrival timing straight off the printed Dates column (instead of
adding the +2-Game-Turn delay from [20.63]/[20.66a]'s own Key) will silently arrive every
Axis Replacement Point two Game-Turns early.

## 4. `[20.67]` — structural note, not a missing chart

Rule text (`20.67`, p.032) reads "Axis Replacement Point Type Limitations Chart (see
Charts and Tables)" as if it were a fourth, separate chart. **It is not.** I searched the
entire `90-charts-tables-and-play-aids.md` for a distinctly-titled `[20.67]` chart and
found none (only three `[20.66]`/`[20.66a]` headings, all on p.176, transcribed above in
full). Re-reading the charts: the per-type, per-turn **Max** and **Dates/Date** columns
*inside* the German and Italian Production Charts already **are** the "Type Limitations" —
20.66 and 20.67 are two case numbers pointing at the same physical chart from two angles
(20.66 = the pool totals, 20.67 = the per-turn-per-type ceiling embedded in the same
table). This is a book cross-referencing quirk, not a gap to fill later.

## 5. OWNER RULING NEEDED — the 20.62 worked example contradicts its own chart

**Reading A (rule 20.62, PDF p.032, verbatim):** "Thus, if the Axis Player wishes to bring
in 10 Italian Infantry Replacement Points, he would need **350 Tons** of Shipping
availability." → implies **35 tons per Italian Infantry Replacement Point**.

**Reading B (the [20.66] Italian Production Chart itself, PDF p.176, verified by direct
zoom of the Tonnage cell — see below):** Italian Infantry Tonnage = **30**. → 10 points =
**300 tons**, not 350.

**Corroborating third source (rule 56.24, PDF p.075, verbatim):** "Thus if the Axis Player
had planned to send 10 Infantry Points in the Game-Turn 55 convoys, this would subtract
**300 tons** from the available tonnage for that Game-Turn." This example is nationality-
unmarked ("Infantry Points," not "Italian Infantry Points"), but it doesn't matter which
side it means — **both** German Infantry (Tonnage=30, `90-charts:5512`) and Italian
Infantry (Tonnage=30, verified above) print the identical rate of 30, so 56.24's "300 tons
for 10 points" is consistent with the chart under either reading and inconsistent with
20.62's own "350."

I zoomed the Italian Infantry Tonnage cell specifically to rule out an OCR mid-column
misread (e.g. "30" vs "35" vs "36") — at 4x magnification on the 300dpi render the digits
are unambiguously **"30"**, cleanly printed with a serif font, no adjacent smudge that
could plausibly be a "5."

**Net: two independent citations (the chart's own Tonnage column, and the Chapter 56
worked example) agree on 30 tons/point; only 20.62's own worked example says 35.** This
looks like a print error in the 20.62 example ("350" for what should almost certainly read
"300" given the chart it's illustrating), but per the port's transcription rules I am not
resolving it — **the fix, if any, is the number the book prints**, and here the book
prints two different numbers for the same fact in two different chapters. Recording both
readings and all three page citations (PDF pp. 032, 075, 176) for an explicit ruling before
any per-point tonnage constant is baked into `data/`.

## 6. Minor flag — a stale/broken internal cross-reference (non-blocking)

**[20.62]** and **[20.64]** both cite "(see Case 51.5)" / "(see Section 51.0)" for the
Shipping Tonnage allowance mechanic. In this edition of the book, **Chapter 51 is STORES**
(`docs/rules/51-stores.md`) and only goes up to case 51.2 — there is no case 51.5, and
Stores has nothing to do with convoy tonnage. The actual Shipping Tonnage
allowance-by-month-and-die-roll mechanic lives in **Chapter 56, The Axis Naval Convoy
System** — specifically **[56.21]/[56.22]** (the Tonnage Capacity Table lookup, worked
example on p.075) and the **[56.5] Axis Convoy Capacity Table** chart. This reads like a
stale cross-reference left over from an earlier draft's chapter numbering (Stores and the
Convoy System may once have been adjacent/merged sections). Doesn't affect any number in
this transcription — flagging only so a future rule-51/56 pass doesn't waste time hunting
for a "51.5" that isn't there.

## 7. Summary for the implementation phase

- **Pool exists, fully transcribed above, verified against the scan, zero numeric
  discrepancies found in the chart itself** (only the cross-chapter 300-vs-350 tonnage-rate
  conflict in §5, which is a rule-text-vs-chart issue, not a chart-transcription error).
- Axis RP tonnage cost = the **Tonnage** column of the relevant Production Chart row (per
  point, constant regardless of which Max/Dates sub-period it's brought in during) — this
  is the number that should seed the real `replacement_point_tons` mechanic that currently
  sits as an unread placeholder in `data/logistics_rates.json:464`. **Pending the §5
  ruling** on the Italian Infantry rate (30 vs 35) before that one specific cell is keyed
  in; every other row has no such conflict.
- Axis RPs additionally: **priority over all other convoy cargo** (20.64), must be
  **planned 2 Game-Turns ahead** (20.63), capped by **both** a campaign-total *and* a
  per-turn-per-type maximum that shifts at named Game-Turn breakpoints (the Max/Dates
  columns), and an Infantry-only **air option at flat 2 tons/point** (54.5, footnote †).
- Commonwealth RPs: **zero tonnage cost, zero convoy representation, zero interdiction
  exposure** (20.75) — planned **4 Game-Turns (2 months)** ahead (20.72) instead of 2, with
  Infantry and Truck points drawn **randomly** rather than at Player discretion (20.73, the
  [20.78B] dice table — not transcribed here, out of scope) and arrival always at
  Cairo/Alexandria (20.76). This is the complete rule-level asymmetry; the Commonwealth
  Production Chart's actual point values ([20.78], `90-charts:3391-3480`) are a separate,
  larger transcription task.
- **[20.67]** needs no separate chart entry in `data/` — it is not a distinct dataset, it's
  the Max/Date columns already captured in §3.
- Not addressed here (explicitly out of scope, flagged as pointers only): **[20.3]**
  Replacement Point Conversion Chart, **[20.78]** Commonwealth Production System charts,
  and any scenario-specific Axis pool sub-caps referenced by 20.61's parenthetical ("Each
  Scenario lists the maximum available for that Scenario, if any") — not located in this
  pass; likely relevant only to the smaller `rommels_arrival`/`siege_of_tobruk` benchmarks,
  not the full `campaign()` scenario, since this chart is explicitly captioned as covering
  "the entire Campaign Game."
