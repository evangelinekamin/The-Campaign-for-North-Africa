## 1. What this rule is

CNA gives most Commonwealth units (never Axis — see the rule's own aside below) a lower
**Untrained** Basic Morale Rating on arrival than the Basic Morale Rating they are
*designed* to fight at. A unit reaches its designed rating only by spending Operations
Stages sitting still, doing nothing else, in a Training Area, with a same-arm instructor
battalion present. This is the ONE exception carved out in 17.1: "Each unit in the game
has a Basic Morale Rating... which, with the exception of Training (Case 17.3), never
changes." Replacement Points (20.43) piggy-back on the identical mechanism for a
different reason: raw Replacement Points aren't under-motivated, they're literally still
being organized/uncrated/assigned before they're usable.

These are two distinct trained-status tracks that happen to share one clock-and-location
mechanic:
- **17.3** — an already-fielded CW *unit* raising its permanent Basic Morale Rating over
  time (an OA-sheet-level, one-time-in-the-unit's-life climb).
- **20.43** — a freshly-arrived *Replacement Point* (Infantry/Gun/Tank/AC/Recce/Commando)
  becoming eligible to move/absorb at all. This has nothing to do with morale — the rule
  says so explicitly (see the chart footnote quoted in §3).

## 2. Rule 17.3 TRAINING — verbatim (docs/rules/17-morale.md, scan pp. 027–028)

> Several of the Commonwealth units arrived in Egypt ill-prepared to fight a desert
> campaign. Therefore, their Morale Rating on arrival is lower than their assigned Basic
> Morale. In order to reach their assigned Basic Morale they must undergo Training.
>
> To forestall questions on the subject, only Commonwealth units must undergo training
> for a variety of reasons. Most German units had already undergone considerable combat
> before arriving in Africa, and, although they were no more desert-acclimated than any
> other forces, this previous experience counted for much, in the designers' minds. As
> for the Italians, most of these units had served in Africa, had seen combat, or are of
> too low a designated morale to bother with training. Those Commonwealth units
> designated for Training are based on unit descriptions and fitness reports in the
> various sources.

**[17.31]** Units that are in need of Training have, on their OA Sheets, two Basic Morale
Ratings. The rating in parentheses is the Untrained Rating; the other Rating is the
maximum level of Training and Experience that that unit may reach, through Training.

**[17.32]** A Commonwealth unit undergoes Training in any one of three areas: Cairo (any
one of the city hexes); Helwan (1430); or Alexandria (any city hex), or Amiriya, Abouqir
or Deghelia.

**[17.33]** To undergo Training a unit may not expend any Capability Points during an
Operations Stage. If, for any reason whatsoever, a unit expends CP's during an
Operations Stage, its Training has been interrupted.

**[17.34]** For every six Operations Stages that a unit undergoes Training, it raises its
Untrained Basic Morale Rating one point. (Thus, a -1 would become a 0, a +1 a +2, etc.)
It may never exceed its designated Basic Morale.

**[17.35]** In order for a unit to undergo Training, a combat battalion which has (or has
reached) its designated Basic Morale must be present in the hex with the Training unit
during each Operations Stage of Training. Infantry divisions or brigades may use an
Infantry-type battalion; any unit with tanks must have a Tank battalion present to
train. Such instruction battalions do not count against stacking limitations, and such
battalions used for instructional purposes must be at least 1/3 of their assigned TOE
Strength. Each instruction battalion may train as many units as may be stacked in that
hex.

**[17.36]** Units are not required to undergo Training; they may enter combat with their
lesser Morale Rating and then be pulled back at a later time for Training. However, only
Training can permanently raise the Basic Morale Rating, regardless of the amount of
combat encountered.

**[17.37]** Commonwealth And Axis (see Case 20.43) Training Areas are indicated on the
game maps.

**[17.4]** THE MORALE MODIFICATION TABLE (see Charts and Tables) — cross-reference only;
this is the *per-combat* Cohesion→Morale-modifier table, a completely different mechanic
(already implemented, see §6). Not part of this chart.

**[17.6]** TRAINING CHART (see Charts and Tables) — transcribed in §3/§4 below.

## 3. Rule 20.43 — verbatim (docs/rules/20-reinforcements-*.md, scan p. 031)

**[20.43]** Replacement Points (both Axis and Commonwealth) are required to undergo
Training. They must train for a specific number of Operations Stages, which may include
the Stage of arrival. Players should consult the rules on Training (17.3) for
particulars, as the same rules that apply to Training Reinforcements apply to
Replacements. Axis points train in Tripoli or any major city. Commonwealth units train
in those locations listed in Case 17.32. When in doubt as to unit size, five Replacement
Points equals one battalion. The schedule for Replacement Training is as follows:

| Type of Replacement Point   | OpStages of Training                          |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Gun Replacement               | One (Actually, not Training; more un-crating) |
| Infantry Replacement          | Three                                          |
| Tank, AC, Recce Replacement   | Six                                             |

> Note: This has nothing to do with Commonwealth training for Morale.

**[20.44]–[20.49]** (context only, not part of the assigned chart, quoted in full for
completeness of the mechanic): Trained Infantry RPs are usually trucked to the Front
after Training and transferred to the assigned unit; Trained Tank/Gun/Recce RPs may move
under their own power, use Railroads (CW), etc. RPs require Stores/Fuel like any unit
when moving. RPs moving toward the Front for assignment are Truck Convoys (Truck Convoy
Movement Phase only). RPs may be absorbed only during the Reorganization Segment, at
which point they acquire the assigned unit's cohesion, breakdown and other levels.

Also relevant (already excerpted in full in 20.42, which 20.43 depends on for the RP
model): RPs "never voluntarily enter an Enemy ZOC unless the hex is occupied by a
Friendly unit"; "never used in any type of combat other than defending against Close
Assault"; "all have a Basic Morale of -3 (in their unabsorbed state)"; "may never exceed
their [CPA]... if they ever exceed their CPA — for any reason — they are considered to
have a Cohesion Level of -26."

## 5. Mechanics implied by the rule text (for the implementation phase; not invented — every clause below cites its case)

For a **Commonwealth unit undergoing 17.3 Training**:
- It needs two Basic Morale numbers per unit: `untrained_morale` (the OA-sheet
  parenthetical, 17.31) and `trained_morale` (the OA-sheet ceiling the unit may reach,
  17.31/17.34's "may never exceed its designated Basic Morale").
- A unit is "in Training" for an Operations Stage iff: it is in one of the six named
  hexes/areas (17.32: Cairo any city hex / Helwan hex 1430 / Alexandria any city hex /
  Amiriya / Abouqir / Deghelia), it spends **zero** CPs that stage (17.33 — any CP
  expenditure for *any* reason interrupts Training, it does not just pause it: the rule
  says "its Training has been interrupted", implying a reset is a plausible reading but
  the rule does not explicitly say the *counter* zeroes vs. merely pausing — **flagging
  this ambiguity**, see §7), and an eligible same-arm instructor battalion (≥1/3 its own
  TOE strength; infantry-type for infantry divisions/brigades, tank-type for any unit
  with tanks) is present in the same hex (17.35).
- Every 6 consecutive (per 17.33/17.34) Training stages raises `untrained_morale` by
  exactly 1, capped at `trained_morale` (17.34, and the [17.6] chart footnote "raise any
  type of an untrained Commonwealth unit's Basic Morale one point").
- Training is optional — a unit may fight at its lower rating indefinitely and be pulled
  back for Training later (17.36); nothing else (combat outcomes, time alone) raises
  Basic Morale.

For a **Replacement Point undergoing 20.43 Training**:
- Every RP (Axis and Commonwealth) starts untrained regardless of type and must sit for a
  fixed OpStage count by Class before it may be assigned/absorbed: Gun 1, Infantry 3,
  Tank/AC/Recce 6, Commando 12 ([17.6]; 20.43's own inline table omits the Commando row).
  The Stage of arrival may count toward this total ("which may include the Stage of
  arrival", 20.43).
- Location constraint differs by side: Axis RPs train in Tripoli or any major city;
  Commonwealth RPs train in the same six locations as 17.32 (20.43 explicitly
  cross-references 17.32 rather than restating it).
- 20.43 explicitly imports "the same rules that apply to Training Reinforcements" — i.e.
  the CP-expenditure interruption (17.33) and instructor-battalion-presence (17.35)
  clauses apply to RP training too, per its own text ("Players should consult the rules
  on Training (17.3) for particulars, as the same rules that apply to Training
  Reinforcements apply to Replacements").
- This RP-Training clock is entirely independent of, and has nothing to do with, CW
  unit-morale Training (20.43's own closing note, and 17.6's footnote covers both uses of
  the identical OpStage-counting mechanism under one chart).

## 6. Current engine state (read-only check, no game/data edits made)

- `game/state.py:49` — `morale: int = 0`, a single fixed field. There is no
  `untrained_morale` / `trained_morale` pair, no training-stage counter, no
  training-location or instructor-battalion check anywhere in `game/`.
  `grep -rn "untrained\|Untrained\|17\.3\|20\.43" game/*.py` — zero hits.

## 7. Flags

- **Not an internal contradiction, but a genuine gap this doc cannot close:** the
  *per-unit* Untrained Basic Morale values (17.31's OA-sheet parenthetical numbers, e.g.
  "which CW units start at what untrained rating, and what their trained ceiling is")
  live on the physical **OA Sheets** — described in `docs/rules/04-game-equipment.md`
  (scan p. 010) as three separate OA Charts (Allied/German/Italian), each subdivided into
  per-formation sheets, listing "the Basic Morale Rating for all of the counters
  comprising that unit (untrained Commonwealth unit's morale at instant of arrival is
  indicated in parentheses where needed)". **These OA Sheets are NOT present in this PDF**
  — I checked `docs/rules/99-foldout-charts.md` (the book's oversized-foldout appendix,
  scan pp. 180-192): those foldouts are the hex-map and physical counter sheets, not the
  OA charts. Per the standing memory `cna-unit-stats-source`, the OA/Unit-Characteristics
  data lives in PNGs inside the VASSAL mod (`tools/vassal/`), a separate physical game
  component from this rulebook scan. **A later phase implementing 17.3/20.43 will need to
  pull the per-unit untrained-rating list from the VASSAL OA-sheet PNGs, not from
  `tmp/The Campaign for North Africa.pdf`** — flagging this as an out-of-scan-scope
  dependency rather than resolving it here.
- **Ambiguity flag (not a book contradiction, a genuine rules-as-written gap I will not
  resolve myself):** 17.33 says CP expenditure during a Training stage "interrupts"
  Training but never states whether an interruption (a) zeroes the accumulated OpStage
  count back to 0, (b) merely fails to credit that one stage while keeping prior progress,
  or (c) permanently disqualifies the unit from ever training again. Cross-reading 20.43
  ("which may include the Stage of arrival") only clarifies RP training's start point, not
  what "interrupted" means for the counter. **OWNER RULING NEEDED** if a later phase
  needs a specific behavior — recommend (b) (fails to credit only that stage; the
  colloquial wargame sense of "interrupted training" is that you resume where you left
  off, not that you start the whole six-stage course over) as the least-punitive faithful
  reading, but the book does not say this outright and I have not adopted it as fact.
- Nothing was unreadable. Every rendered page (027, 028, 031, 100) was crisp at 300dpi and
  matched the existing OCR transcription exactly — no OCR corrections needed for this
  chart, unlike the 54.17 class of errors.
- Rule 60.41/60.47 (`docs/rules/60-scenario-group-one-the-italians.md`) name specific
  scenario-start "In Training" CW units (e.g. "Cairo and/or Helwan: In Training: 6th
  Australian Division...") and remind the player "some units need Training, which means
  that other units have to be assigned as Training Units" — confirms 17.35's instructor
  requirement is load-bearing at scenario setup, not just a flavor clause. Not
  transcribed in full here (out of assigned scope) but flagged as the scenario-layer
  companion data a later phase will also need.
