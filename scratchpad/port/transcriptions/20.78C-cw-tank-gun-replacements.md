**Key/footnote, quoted verbatim (PDF p.141):**

> **#** = Total number of replacement points of that type that may be brought in. **Max** = maximum
> number of replacement points of that type that may be brought in per Game-Turn (however, see below).
> **Date(s)** = Nr/Nr: First/last Game-Turn on which the replacement point may be planned. **\*** = The
> maximum number of points of that type that may be brought in per month (Note: by months, not four
> week cycles from original week of availability). **†** = The maximum number that may be brought in
> every two weeks (i.e., in the period of the first and second weeks of a month and the period of the
> third and fourth weeks of a month).
>
> Note that the "Tiger" Convoy (GT 32), a shipment of tanks and planes, is in addition to any
> replacements listed on this chart.

**Reading the columns (this is the whole mechanic):**

- **`#` is a hard campaign-wide cap.** Over the entire game the CW Player may draw at most that many
  Replacement Points of that type. This is the *"functions like the Axis Pools"* pool total ([20.73];
  cf. [20.61] for the Axis).
- **`Max` is a per-Game-Turn rate cap** — how many of that type may arrive in any one Game-Turn —
  **except** when marked:
  - **`*`** → the number is a **per-month** cap instead (calendar months, *not* rolling four-week
    windows from the item's first availability). Twelve of the 24 items are `*`.
  - **`†`** → **per-two-weeks** cap (weeks 1-2 of a month, and weeks 3-4). Only Crusader Mk III (6†).
  - Churchill's Max is **`–`** (dash): only 1 exists, so no rate cap is meaningful.
- **`Date(s)`** is the window in which the item may be **planned** (not the arrival turn — see §3):
  - **single number** (e.g. Sherman `89`) → first Game-Turn it may be planned; runs to campaign end
    (GT111) with no printed last-turn cutoff.
  - **`Nr/Nr`** (e.g. Crusader Mk I `31/76`) → first/last Game-Turn it may be planned. The windowed
    items are the obsolescing types — the pre-Crusader cruisers/light tanks all cut off at GT38, the
    Matilda at GT74, etc.

## 3. Production / lead-time mechanics (rule 20.7, PDF p.032)

**[20.73]** (verbatim): *"The Commonwealth Production System functions like the Axis Pools except for
Infantry and Truck Points."* So **everything on [20.78C] is Axis-Pool-style**: the CW Player draws it
**at will** — no die roll (unlike Infantry [20.78B] and Trucks [20.78A], which *are* rolled) — within
three limits at once: the `#` campaign total, the `Max` per-turn/month/fortnight rate, and the
`Date(s)` planning window.

**No shipping / tonnage cost — [20.75]** (verbatim): *"Replacement Points listed in the Reinforcement
Schedule arrive in addition to any Production. The Commonwealth Player has no Shipping Problems; his
Replacement Points simply arrive."* Contrast the Axis, whose every RP is billed against convoy tonnage
([20.62], e.g. 10 Italian Infantry RP = 350 tons) and exposed to Malta interdiction. **This is the
structural asymmetry the chart encodes** — the CW's late-war armour flood costs nothing to ship.

**Lead time — [20.72]** (verbatim, PDF p.032): *"The Commonwealth Player must plan **two months in
advance** (unless specified in a given Scenario) for the arrival of Replacement Points via Production.
Thus, for Points required for August, 1942, the Commonwealth Player must plan — using the Production
Systems — in the first Naval Convoy Schedule Phase of July, 1942. However, the Commonwealth Player
always uses the Production Table for the month (one month[s] in the future) in which he wishes his
units to arrive — not the month in which he is consulting it."*

**OWNER RULING NEEDED — the equipment lead time (governs the exact Sherman arrival GT).** The
[20.78C] `Date(s)` footnote says Date = the Game-Turn on which an item may be **planned**, so arrival
= plan-turn + lead. But the lead is textually tangled (this is the *same* tension the sibling
`20.78B` doc flags for Infantry, here re-flagged for equipment because it changes the Sherman clock):
- **[20.72]** states the CW production lead as **two months** (≈ 8 Game-Turns; a GT is one week).
- **[20.72]'s own worked example** (plan in July → arrive August) demonstrates only **one month**
  (≈ 4 GT).
- **[20.21]** and **[20.78B]** state a **four-Game-Turn** lead — but explicitly for
  *Infantry* Production, which **[20.73]** carves *out of* the "functions like the Axis Pools" clause.
- **[20.63]** (the Axis Pool that [20.73] says CW equipment mimics) uses a **two-full-Game-Turn** lead.

Readings on the page, not reconciled by the book:
  - **Reading A** (the sibling doc's default, carried here): [20.78C] equipment uses [20.72]'s
    ~2-month (~8 GT) lead; Infantry/Trucks use their specifically-stated 4-GT lead. No contradiction —
    two production families, two leads.
  - **Reading B**: [20.72]'s worked example is authoritative and the lead is ~1 month (~4 GT) for
    everything.
  - **Reading C**: "functions like the Axis Pools" pulls in [20.63]'s 2-GT lead for equipment.
  - Pages: [20.72]/[20.73] PDF p.032; [20.21]/[20.43] PDF p.031. All read directly off the scan.

**This is unresolved by me and must be ruled before the equipment clock is coded**, because it moves
every arrival date on the chart by up to ~6 Game-Turns.

**Arrival location — [20.76]** (verbatim): *"All Planned Replacements arrive in any hex in Cairo or
Alexandria. They, like other Replacements, must undergo Training (see Case 20.43)."*

**Training — [20.43]** (verbatim schedule, PDF p.031; fully transcribed at
`scratchpad/port/transcriptions/17.3-20.43-training.md`, cross-referenced not duplicated):

| Type of Replacement Point | OpStages of Training |
| --- | --- |
| Gun Replacement | One (*"Actually, not Training; more un-crating"*) |
| Infantry Replacement | Three |
| Tank, AC, Recc Replacement | Six |

So **every gun/AA item** on [20.78C] (Armored Recce/AC counts as AC → six; the 25-pdr, 4.5"/5.5"/155mm,
2-pdr, 6-pdr, SP 6-pdr, 17-pdr, and both AA rows are **Gun** → **one OpStage**), and **every tank**
(Mark VI Light through Churchill → **six OpStages**). Note the Armored Recce/Armored Car row is
AC-class, hence six OpStages, not one.

**Movement to the front — [20.44]**: Trained **Tank, Gun and Recce** Replacement Points *"may move on
their own, use Railroads (Commonwealth), etc."* — unlike Infantry RP, which are trucked ([20.46]:
Infantry RP move only in the Truck Convoy Movement Phase). Absorbed only in the Reorganization Segment
([20.49]), acquiring the host unit's cohesion/breakdown levels.

**Not on this chart, deliberately:** the **Bishop** SP 25-pounder (see the [4.47] chart note,
`docs/rules/90-...:2412`): *"These self-propelled artillery TOE Strength Points are not listed on the
Commonwealth Production Chart as they are 'free.'"* Bishops arrive by refitting an RHA regiment
(four continuous Game-Turns in Cairo), not via [20.78C]. Likewise the Scorpion/Baron/Crab mine-clearers
(`docs/rules/90-...:3307`) *"do not arrive via the Commonwealth Production schedule"* — they come with
two returning tank battalions in Oct 1942 (El Alamein set-up).

**The Sherman is the sharpest tooth on the chart: 62 Replacement Points, Max 12 per *Game-Turn*
(not per month — Sherman's Max carries no `*`), first plannable GT89, no shipping cost, no last-turn
cutoff.** With a 12/GT rate the full 62 can be on the map within ~6 Game-Turns of first availability.
**Note for "get the Sherman clock exact":** the book's `89` is the **first plan turn**, so the true
on-map date is `89 + lead time` under whichever §3 reading is ruled — the port plan's shorthand
"62 Shermans from GT89" treats 89 as the arrival turn and therefore reads the Shermans ~4-8 GT
*early*. The Crusader Mk III (6/two-weeks from GT88) and 17-pdr (3/GT from GT103) round out the
late-war upgrade; the pre-Crusader cruisers and Mark VI Light are early-war only (all cut at GT38).

## 5. Tie to victory — [64.74] unused Replacement Points (PDF p.088, read on the scan)

**[64.74]** (verbatim, confirmed against the render, crop `render/p88_overview.png` bottom-right;
identical to the OCR at `docs/rules/64-...:81`):

> **[64.74]** The next series of points are replacement Victory Points. Each Player receives **one
> point for each unused Replacement Point** allotted to him in his Production Charts, **not including
> planes and/or Trucks** and, **for the Commonwealth Player only, Infantry**. Thus, if the
> Commonwealth Player had a total of 35 Crusader I Tank Replacement Points available for the game, but
> he uses only 18, he gains 17 points.

**What this means for [20.78C]:** every Replacement Point on this chart that the CW Player does **not**
spend by game end is worth **1 Victory Point**. The exclusions (planes, Trucks, and — CW only —
Infantry) mean **[20.78C] is exactly the chart 64.74 scores for the Commonwealth**: all 24 rows here
are tanks/guns/AA (no planes, no trucks, no infantry), so **the full 958-point pool is 64.74-eligible**.
The worked example is a **cross-check that both this chart and the 64.74 OCR are correct**: "35
Crusader I ... uses only 18, gains 17" — 35 − 18 = 17, and **35 is exactly this chart's Crusader Mk I
`#`.** This is a self-consistency proof between p.088 and p.141.

**Design tension worth flagging to the implementer (not a rule dispute):** 64.74 rewards *hoarding*
armour RP (unused = VP) while the whole point of the chart is to *spend* them replacing losses. The
CW Player trades battlefield strength for VP at 1:1 on this pool. `game/campaign_victory.py` already
carries a docstring noting the 64.74 unused-RP VPs are **deferred/unbuilt** — so neither the pool nor
its VP scoring exists yet; both land together when rule-20 is ported.

## 7. Summary for the implementation phase

- **Draw model:** Axis-Pool-style, **no die roll** ([20.73]). The CW Player elects each turn how many
  of each type to plan, bounded simultaneously by `#` (campaign total), `Max` (per-turn, or per-month
  if `*`, or per-two-weeks if `†`), and the `Date(s)` planning window.
- **Lead time:** **OWNER RULING NEEDED** — [20.72] "two months" vs. its own 1-month example vs.
  [20.63]'s 2-GT Axis-Pool lead (§3). Moves every arrival GT, including the Sherman's, by up to ~6 GT.
- **Cost:** **none** — no tonnage, no shipping, no interdiction ([20.75]). The CW's structural
  advantage.
- **Destination:** Cairo or Alexandria, then **Training** ([20.43]: Gun = 1 OpStage; Tank/AC/Recce =
  6 OpStages), then self-move/rail to the front ([20.44]), absorbed in Reorganization ([20.49]).
- **Victory tie:** every **unused** RP on this chart = **1 CW VP** ([64.74]); the whole 958-point pool
  is eligible (planes/Trucks/Infantry excluded, none of which are on this chart).
- **Scenario variant:** nine items reduced by [64.54.C] for the Desert-Fox-start campaign (§6);
  full campaign uses §1 unchanged.
- **The Sherman clock:** 62 RP, **Max 12 per Game-Turn**, first plan-GT **89**, no cutoff, free to
  ship — the decisive late-war medium; true on-map date = 89 + the §3 lead time.
- **Nothing in `game/` reads or models any of this** — the rule-20 RP economy is unbuilt (sibling
  doc §0). This is a from-scratch transcription.
