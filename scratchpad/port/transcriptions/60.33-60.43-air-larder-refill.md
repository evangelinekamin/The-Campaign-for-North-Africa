## 2. The larder that drains — [36.17], PDF p.053 (verbatim, read on scan)

> **[36.17]** An airfield is a supply dump for supplies to be used by the SGSU's on that airfield.
> Fuel, ammunition, stores, etc., may be stored at an airfield as if it were a dump. Land units may
> not use airfield supply dumps unless it is an emergency. (Exactly what constitutes an emergency is
> left to the Player.) Any SGSU at an airfield may make use of the supplies there to maintain and
> ready its planes.

So the "air larder" **is a supply dump co-located with the field** — the same object as any land
dump, restricted to air use (land units only in emergency). What draws it down:

- **[35.14] SGSU self-upkeep** (verbatim, PDF p.053): *"Each SGSU must expend **one Stores Point per
  Game-Turn**. In addition, each SGSU requires **one Fuel Point and one Water Point per Operations
  Stage**. SGSUs without the required supplies (for themselves) may not repair their planes.
  (Maintaining the planes will, of course, require additional supplies of Fuel and Ammunition)."*
  Engine constants: `game/air.py` `SGSU_STORES_PER_TURN=1`, `SGSU_FUEL_PER_STAGE=1`,
  `SGSU_WATER_PER_STAGE=1`.
- **[38.21]/[38.22] refuelling** (verbatim, PDF p.054): *"Planes must have fuel to fly. The amount of
  fuel needed to refuel a plane is its Fuel Consumption Rating... the number of Fuel Points required
  to enable one plane of that type to fly any one mission."* — *"Planes are refueled at air facilities
  by SGSU's."*
- **[38.36] refit** charges a Stores Point per refit **attempt** (the second, engine-measured half of
  the drain — see the engine-status note above).
- **[41.x] arming** draws Ammunition for bombing / air-to-air missions.

All of these are billed against the co-located 36.17 dump.

## 3. The faucet — the three transcribed refill paths

### (a) [35.15] First Line Transport — trucks attached to an SGSU (PDF p.053, verbatim, read on scan)

> **[35.15]** Truck units may be attached to an SGSU as First Line Transport. They are used to carry
> the supplies that the SGSU needs to keep its planes fit and readied.

This is the primary faucet: lorries (the [60.33]/[60.43] "Any Air Facility" points) attach to a
squadron's SGSU and **haul the SGSU's upkeep + its planes' fuel/ammo** from a source dump into the
field. Combined-arms cross-refs on the same page: **[35.11]** SGSUs *"are the mechanics, trucks,
equipment, etc."*; **[35.12]** SGSUs are themselves vehicles that *"move in the Truck Convoy Phase"*.
So the whole air-logistics tail moves in the Truck Convoy Movement Phase, exactly as the ground
truck-convoy resupply does.

### (b) [36.3] Flying boat basins — trucks suffice, no tender (PDF p.054, verbatim, read on scan)

> **[36.3]** Flying boat basins are "airfields" for flying boat seaplanes. All flying boats *must* use
> a flying boat basin (or alighting area) for landing and maintenance. They may not use airfields or
> landing strips. Basins have the same features as an airfield, with the exception that their Capacity
> is *three* Squadrons. Construction details are in Case 24.7. **Players should note that they can
> bring supplies to a flying boat basin simply by bringing trucks into the hex; they need no other
> transport.** Flying boat basins are placed in any coastal hex. As with airfields, flying boat basins
> have one intrinsic Strength Point of anti-air (see Case 36.18). Flying boat basins may not be used
> for normal aircraft.

The load-bearing clause is bolded: a **basin is refilled by trucks like any other dump** — the book
pre-empts the natural assumption that a seaplane base needs a special tender/lighter. So both the
land field (36.17) and the water basin (36.3) share the *same* refill mechanic: drive trucks into the
hex. (A flying boat **alighting area**, [36.4], is *"the same as flying boat basins, except ... a
capacity of one Squadron"* — so it refills identically.)

### (c) [36.5] Off-map air facilities — an infinite larder, no faucet needed (PDF p.054, verbatim)

> **[36.5]** There are several off-map air facilities: e.g., the Tunisia boxes, the Commonwealth
> facilities such as Deversoir, on map E, et cetera. These are treated the same as other, on-map
> facilities with three exceptions: **a) There are unlimited supplies for airplane maintenance and
> repair;** b) They may exceed the SGSU limit up to the limit listed on the field; 3) They may never
> be destroyed by bombing (although they may be reduced to zero), and may be rebuilt using the Malta
> Air Facility Construction Table (one roll gives total levels for all CW air facilities each
> game-turn). ... Axis Mediterranean bases are also different in one other way: they may not be bombed.

So the **Delta/Canal-Zone rear fields (Deversoir, Abu Seier, Fayid, Ismailia, Kabrit, Port Said) and
the Axis Tripoli/Tunisia boxes have a bottomless larder** — no faucet required, they never run dry.
[61.44] restates the Axis half in passing (*"Tripoli/Tunisia air facilities have unlimited Fuel and
Ammo"*). These are unbuilt in the engine only because it has no off-map air box to stand them in
(see `data/air_facilities_60_5.json` `_off_map`), not because the rule is unclear.

**Where supply comes FROM before the trucks carry it:** the source is the ordinary supply net — ports
([55]), the Axis convoy system ([56]), the Commonwealth base ([57]), co-ordinated by [54]. An airfield
being *"a supply dump ... as if it were a dump"* (36.17) means it sits on that net like any dump; the
[60.33]/[60.43] trucks are the last-mile carriers, and [60.34]/[60.44]'s *"freely distributed among
his airfields"* pools ([60.34]: 1200 Ammo / 850 Fuel / 100 Stores / 100 Water for the Axis;
[60.44]: 200 Ammo / 250 Fuel / 50 Stores for the Commonwealth) are the **initial** larder fills the
faucet is meant to keep topped up.

## 4. Summary for the implementation phase

- **The larder** = a 36.17 supply dump co-located with each on-map air facility (and a basin per
  36.3). It drains via 35.14 SGSU upkeep (1 Stores/GT + 1 Fuel + 1 Water/OpStage per SGSU), 38.21/24
  plane refuelling, 38.36 refit-attempt Stores, and 41.x arming.
- **The faucet** (this doc's subject) = trucks. Three transcribed paths: **35.15** first-line trucks
  attached to an SGSU; **36.3** trucks driven into a basin hex (explicitly "no other transport");
  **36.5(a)** off-map fields have *unlimited* supply and need no faucet. The lorries that do the
  hauling are the **[60.33] "Any Air Facility" (10 L / 50 M)** and **[60.43] "Any Air Facility"
  (5 L / 30 M / 20 H)** allotments (plus the general pools).
- **Engine gap, precisely:** the faucet is *built* (`game.relay.air_supply_orders`) but *under-plumbed*
  — no truck-park attachment to dispersed fields (`game/air.py:88-89`), and the 38.36 compulsory-refit
  drain has no owner order-channel (`game/air.py:91-100`). The **lorry allotments and the refill rules
  themselves are not in `data/`** (the sibling `data/air_facilities_60_5.json` names them as debt).
- **No book contradictions found** in [60.33]/[60.43], 35.14, 35.15, 36.3, 36.5, 36.17 — all read
  clean off the scan and match the OCR verbatim. No owner ruling needed for this chart.
