## 1. Section 8.6 BREAKING OFF (rules 8.61-8.68, PDF p.15)

Quoted verbatim from the scan:

> **[8.61]** Breaking Off is a function of Movement that occurs when a unit begins a Friendly Movement
> Segment in *Contact* or *Engaged* with an Enemy Combat unit. Contact and Engaged markers are
> provided to indicate such status. (There has been some discussion of this in 8.24; see also Case
> 15.81).
>
> **[8.62]** Units are in *Contact* if they are in an Enemy Zone of Control at the beginning of a
> Movement Segment.
>
> **[8.63]** Units are *Engaged* directly as a result of Close Assault; *Engaged* is a combat result.
> Engaged units do not necessarily have to be in Enemy Zones of Control.
>
> **[8.64]** A unit in Contact or Engaged with an Enemy combat unit at the beginning of a Movement
> Segment or a Retreat Before Assault (not Reaction) may not move until it has paid a cost in CP's to
> break off such contact. In addition, First Line Trucks wishing to detach from an Engaged or
> Contacted parent unit must expend the necessary CP's to so detach. Second/Third Line Trucks wishing
> to attach as First Line Trucks to Engaged or Contacted units do not expend additional CP's to so
> attach.
>
> **[8.65]** A unit in Contact must expend two CP's to Break Off before moving.
>
> **[8.66]** A unit that is Engaged must expend four CP's to Break Off before moving.
>
> **[8.67]** When all of the Friendly units that were in Contact or Engaged with an Enemy combat unit
> Break Off that Enemy unit is no longer Engaged or in Contact.
>
> **[8.68]** Contact and Engaged markers affect only the combat units in the hex at the time of their
> placement. Units moving into a hex with a Contact or Engaged marker are not affected by that marker.

### Procedure, restated as an algorithm

1. At the start of a unit's Movement Segment (or a Retreat Before Assault that is not a Reaction —
   8.64 explicitly excludes Reaction, which per 8.52 never pays a break-off cost), classify the unit:
   - **Engaged** if it carries an Engaged marker (placed as a Close Assault result, 8.63/15.81; cleared
     end of Operations Stage per 15.81).
   - Else **Contact** if it occupies a hex under enemy ZOC at that instant (8.62).
   - Else neither — moves freely, no break-off charge.
2. A Contact unit must pay **2 CP** before its first hex of movement out of that ZOC; an Engaged unit
   must pay **4 CP** (8.65/8.66). This is a one-time toll to leave, not per-hex.
3. First Line Trucks detaching from a Contact/Engaged parent pay their own CP toll to detach (8.64
   sentence 2) — a separate charge from the parent's break-off charge. Second/Third Line Trucks
   *attaching* to become First Line Trucks of a Contact/Engaged unit pay **nothing extra** (8.64
   sentence 3) — attaching is free regardless of status; only *detaching from* / *breaking off* a
   Contact/Engaged unit costs CP.
4. Once every Friendly unit that had been in Contact/Engaged with a given Enemy unit has broken off,
   that Enemy unit's Contact/Engaged status is cleared (8.67) — this is a per-(Friendly-unit,
   Enemy-unit) relationship reduced to a per-Enemy-unit marker only once ALL the Friendly parties have
   left.
5. Markers are scoped to the units present at the moment they were placed (8.68) — a unit moving into
   an already-Contact/Engaged hex after the fact inherits nothing.

## 2. Section 10.3 ZOC COMBAT REQUIREMENTS / "HOLDING OFF" — the Mandatory Attack (rules 10.31-10.36, PDF p.18)

Quoted verbatim from the scan (identical to OCR):

> ## 10.3 ZOC COMBAT REQUIREMENTS (HOLDING OFF)
>
> **[10.31]** All hexes containing Enemy units exerting Zones of Control on Friendly combat units must
> be attacked — either by Artillery Barrage or by Close Assault.
>
> Note: A Probe (see Case 15.9) satisfies this requirement if the *basic* differential is −4 or
> better.
>
> **[10.32]** As an exception to 10.31, if the *Friendly* units in such Enemy ZOC consist solely of
> Artillery, anti-tank, or anti-aircraft (or non-combat) units, or there are other type units but they
> are pinned (12.42), these units do not have to attack as in Case 10.31.
>
> **[10.33]** Players may use a special "Holding Off" Artillery Barrage to satisfy the requirements of
> 10.31. (For experienced gamers, this is a sophisticated version of "Soaking Off".) Thus a Friendly
> Division that is in the ZOC of Enemy units from two different hexes may choose to Hold Off one of
> those hexes and Close Assault the other.
>
> **[10.34]** To perform a Holding Off Barrage, a Friendly Player must Barrage an Enemy hex with at
> least as many *Actual* Barrage Strength Points (i.e., Barrage Rating × TOE Strength ÷ 10) as there
> are individual non-Gun (i.e., Artillery, Anti-Tank or AA units) Enemy battalion-equivalent combat
> units (units with less than 1 Stacking Point and Gun units do not count) in that hex. Remember, an
> Enemy Division may consist of, say, eleven battalion-equivalent units, so the Friendly Barraging
> Player would need 11 Actual Barrage Points to perform a Holding Off action against such a Division.
>
> **[10.35]** If, according to the precepts of 10.34, a Friendly Phasing Player can not Hold Off Enemy
> units in a given hex he must Close Assault that hex. (Remember Case 10.32). There is no minimum
> point requirement for a Close Assault.
>
> **[10.36]** If, for any reason, a unit can neither Assault nor Hold Off an Enemy unit, that Friendly
> unit must Retreat three hexes (playing all CP's for such movement) and earn three Disorganization
> Points, in addition to any garnered by the retreat. Such retreat must place the retreating unit
> three hexes distant from the Enemy unit (no doubling back, etc.) and may not be made through any
> hexes in Enemy Zones of Control. If forced to enter an Enemy ZOC the unit Surrenders in entirety.

### Procedure, restated as an algorithm (one hex at a time, evaluated for the Phasing Player during the Combat Segment per rule 11.0's PROCEDURE, which cross-references this Section)

For **every** hex a Friendly (evaluate for whichever side is Phasing that segment — 10.31 says
"Friendly combat units," and 11.0's PROCEDURE says "the Phasing Player must attack all Enemy-occupied
hexes which have units exerting Zones of Control upon Friendly units," so in practice this rule binds
the Phasing Player toward Enemy-controlled hexes) occupies that is under enemy ZOC:

1. **Exemption check (10.32).** If every Friendly combat unit in that hex is Artillery, Anti-Tank, or
   AA (or non-combat), OR any other-type units present are Pinned (12.44 — see discrepancy flag below),
   the hex is exempt: no attack obligation at all. Skip to next hex.
2. **Choose Hold Off or Close Assault (10.33).** Not exempt: the Friendly player picks, hex by hex,
   whether to satisfy 10.31 via a Holding-Off Barrage or a Close Assault. A single Friendly formation
   under simultaneous ZOC from two different Enemy hexes may Hold Off one and Close Assault the other
   — the choice is per Enemy-controlling-hex, not all-or-nothing for the formation.
3. **Holding-Off Barrage sizing (10.34).** To Hold Off a given Enemy hex, count the Enemy's individual
   non-Gun battalion-equivalent combat units in that hex — i.e. every Enemy combat unit in the hex
   EXCEPT: (a) Gun-class units (Artillery, Anti-Tank — note AA units are explicitly *included* in the
   count per 10.34's own wording "non-Gun (i.e., Artillery, Anti-Tank or AA units)" — re-read: 10.34
   actually defines "Gun" as Artillery/Anti-Tank/AA collectively and excludes ALL THREE from the count,
   consistent with 10.32's parallel list; see literal-reading note below), and (b) units with less
   than 1 Stacking Point (company/battery-sized, per 9.25). The Friendly Barraging Player must commit
   Actual Barrage Points (Barrage Rating × TOE Strength ÷ 10, rule 11.32) **at least equal to** that
   count. This is a Holding-Off Barrage under the ordinary Barrage rules (12.0) — "'Holding-off'
   Barrages are resolved in the same fashion as normal Barrage" (12.0's own General Rule note,
   `docs/rules/12-barrage-artillery-combat.md:16`, scan-verified on p.20: *"Note: 'Holding-off'
   Barrages are resolved in the same fashion as normal Barrage (see Case 10.3)."*) — it draws on the
   Artillery Barrage Table (12.6) exactly like a normal Barrage; the only thing "Holding Off" changes
   is that meeting the point threshold discharges the 10.31 attack obligation instead of (or alongside)
   inflicting casualties.
   - **Literal-reading flag:** 10.34's parenthetical is easy to misread. Restated precisely: the units
     that COUNT toward the threshold are the Enemy hex's individual battalion-equivalent combat units
     MINUS (Artillery units, Anti-Tank units, AA units, and sub-Stacking-Point units). Read this
     together with 10.32, whose exemption list is the mirror image (Friendly Artillery/AT/AA/non-combat
     units are themselves exempt from HAVING to attack) — the chart's intent is clearly "Gun-class and
     below-battalion units don't drive (or don't owe) the Holding-Off math," but the parenthetical's
     placement of "AA" inside the "i.e." list reads as if AA units both trigger AND are excluded from
     the same count. There is only one plausible reading (AA is a Gun-class-adjacent exclusion, not a
     double-counted category), so this is **not** an OWNER RULING contradiction, but the phrasing is
     flagged for whoever encodes the counting predicate — get a second pass on it against 3.5 (unit
     characteristics) if code review flags it as ambiguous.
4. **Fallback to Close Assault (10.35).** If the Friendly player cannot muster enough Actual Barrage
   Points to Hold Off a given Enemy hex (per 10.34's threshold), that hex MUST be Close Assaulted
   instead (unless 10.32's exemption already applies). Close Assault carries no minimum Actual
   Strength Point requirement to attempt (a Probe is legal — see 10.31's Note: a Probe with basic
   Assault Differential of −4 or better also discharges the obligation).
5. **Forced retreat if neither is possible (10.36).** If a Friendly unit can do NEITHER — cannot
   Hold Off (insufficient Barrage Points AND not exempt) NOR Close Assault/Probe (e.g., already
   Pinned and thus barred from Close Assault per 12.44, yet not qualifying for the 10.32 Pinned
   exemption because... — the rule says "for any reason", so this is a residual catch-all, not a
   narrowly enumerated trigger list):
   a. The unit must **Retreat exactly three hexes**, spending its ENTIRE remaining CP allotment on
      that movement ("playing all CP's for such movement" — read together with 6.13/6.21, this is a
      forced expenditure, not a cap: if 3 hexes of movement costs more CP than the unit has left, it
      still must be attempted and will run the unit over its CPA, triggering ordinary 6.21
      Disorganization-from-overage on top of the flat penalty in (b)).
   b. The unit earns a **flat +3 Disorganization Points**, on top of (i.e., additively with, not
      instead of) whatever DP the retreat itself generates from CPA overage under 6.21 ("in addition
      to any garnered by the retreat").
   c. The retreat's destination must be **exactly 3 hexes distant** from the triggering Enemy unit,
      measured with no "doubling back" (i.e., a real net displacement of 3, not a there-and-back path
      that nets less).
   d. The retreat path **may not pass through any hex in an Enemy Zone of Control**.
   e. If the unit is **forced to enter an Enemy ZOC** anyway (i.e., no legal 3-hexes-away, ZOC-free
      destination exists), it **Surrenders in entirety** rather than completing any partial retreat.

### Cross-reference discrepancy: "pinned (12.42)"

10.32's own text (scan-verified, PDF p.18, and matching OCR) cites `(12.42)` for the definition of
"pinned." **The scan (PDF p.20, rendered and read) shows Pinned is actually defined at [12.44]**:
*"If a result of 'P' is obtained, the entire target unit is 'Pinned'. Pinned units may not move for
the remainder of that Combat Segment, nor may they engage in Anti-Armor Fire or Close Assault..."*
— `[12.42]` is a completely different rule (*"The Artillery Barrage Table uses a two-dice sequential
read-out..."*, the CRT's die-roll notation convention). **This is the book's own cross-reference,
printed wrong** (confirmed identically in both OCR and scan, so it's not a transcription slip — the
1979 printing itself misdirects). FLAG, not OWNER RULING NEEDED (there is exactly one place "Pinned"
is defined in the rulebook — 12.44 — so there's no genuine ambiguity, just a wrong page number to
chase down if someone follows the citation literally).

### Already in the engine / gap assessment

**Nothing.** Searched `game/*.py` for `mandatory.?attack`, `must.?attack`, `holding.?off`,
`hold.?off`, and the rule numbers 10.31-10.36: zero hits outside this transcription. `game/zoc.py`
implements 10.1/10.2 (which hexes exert/are controlled by a ZOC, and the movement-time consequences —
10.22-10.26) faithfully, and 8.64-8.66's break-off CP toll is wired into movement reachability (see
Section 1 above) — but the requirement that the Phasing Player's units in an Enemy ZOC hex must
actually attack it (Barrage, Hold Off, Close Assault, or forced retreat-and-possible-surrender) has
**no corresponding code path**. `game/engine.py::_combat`'s per-segment order loop
(`combat_orders = policies[side].combat(r.state, side)`, line ~3896) accepts whatever the policy
proposes; there is no validation step comparing "hexes under enemy ZOC this segment" against "hexes
the policy actually ordered an attack/Hold-Off/retreat against" and no fallback that fires 10.36 when
a policy simply omits one. This matches the task's framing: 10.31-10.36 is "the operational friction
that is entirely missing today" — the engine currently lets a unit sit in an Enemy ZOC and do nothing,
which the rulebook forbids outright.

Implementation will need, at minimum:
- A per-Combat-Segment enumeration of "Friendly hexes under Enemy ZOC this segment" (this already
  exists implicitly — `tactics.enemy_zoc_and_occupied` builds the control map both sides use for
  movement; the same `zoc.control_map` output is the right input here).
- A per-hex resolution of exempt (10.32) / Hold-Off-eligible (10.34's Barrage-point accounting,
  which needs a live count of "Enemy individual non-Gun battalion-equivalent units in that hex" —
  the Stacking Point / Gun-class predicates 10.34 needs are the same primitives `game/zoc.py`
  already carries on its `ZocUnit` protocol, e.g. `stacking_points`) / must-Close-Assault (10.35)
  / forced-retreat-and-possible-surrender (10.36) outcome.
- A forced-retreat mover that is NOT ordinary voluntary movement or Retreat Before Assault: it must
  spend the unit's full remaining CP, ignore stacking-negotiated destinations in favor of "exactly 3
  hexes away, no ZOC hexes en route," and route to a Surrender if no such destination exists. None of
  the existing retreat code paths (`Retreat Before Assault`, Close Assault CRT-mandated retreat) match
  this shape; it is closest in spirit to `zoc.reachable_with_zoc` with `enemy_zoc` as a hard block
  rather than a soft stop-on-entry, plus an exact-distance-3 destination filter, which is new search
  logic, not a parameter tweak to the existing one.
- A Surrender trigger distinct from the existing `_resolve_surrender` (which is driven off the 17.x
  Close-Assault-morale path, `game/engine.py:4392`) — 10.36e's surrender has nothing to do with morale
  or Close Assault outcome; it fires purely off "no legal retreat destination exists."

## 3. Rule 11.25 — CP expenditure applies to the whole hex, not just participating units (PDF p.19)

Full 11.2 section quoted for context (all scan-verified against the OCR, no discrepancies):

> ## 11.2 CAPABILITY POINT EXPENDITURES FOR COMBAT
>
> **[11.21]** A non-Phasing unit expends three CP's if it undergoes an attack. Exception: see Case
> 11.27. This will occur under the following circumstances:
>
> a. If it Barrages an Enemy (Phasing) unit; or
> b. If it undergoes any form of Assault (Anti-Tank, Close) except Probe (2 CP's). This includes the
>    situation where a unit Retreats Before Assault into a Friendly hex that is later assaulted.
> c. If it undergoes a Holding Off Barrage (see Case 10.3).
>
> **[11.22]** A Phasing unit expends five CP's to perform any form of attack, other than a Probe. It
> can never expend more than five CP's in a given Combat, although if it engages in a different (new)
> round of combat after completing the first round of combat it will (possibly) expend more CP's. The
> following is a list of circumstances under which a combat unit expends 5 CP's to attack:
>
> 1. It Barrages an Enemy unit; or
> 2. It engages in Anti-Armor or Close Assault.
>
> **[11.23]** A Phasing unit expends fewer than 5 CP's under the following circumstances:
>
> 1. It Probes an Enemy unit (2 CP's), or
> 2. It undergoes Barrage (3 CP's).
>
> **[11.24]** A non-Phasing unit never expends more than three CP's in combat in a given Combat
> Segment, regardless of what happens to it in that Segment. A Phasing unit never expends more than
> five CP's in a given Combat Segment. See Case 11.27.
>
> **[11.25]** CP expenditures apply to all units in a hex, even if certain units did not participate
> in combat in any way. This applies to Phasing and non-Phasing units. Thus, if the Commonwealth
> Player Close Assaults with his entire 7th Armored Division, but chooses to withhold the 1st KRRC,
> the entire division expends five CP's, including the non-participating 1st KRRC. Concurrently, if
> the Artillery units of the 7th Armored Division had barraged Enemy units and then the 7th Armored
> did nothing else, the entire division (if it were all in the same hex) would still expend 5 CP's.
>
> **[11.26]** Generally, Phasing units will expend 5 CP's and non-Phasing units 3 CP's in a given
> combat Segment, except where a Phasing unit chooses to Probe. However, remember, if units in the
> hex with the Probing units Barrage the Enemy units those units expend 5 CP's, regardless of the
> Probe attempt.
>
> **[11.27]** A unit defending against a full (non-Probe) Close Assault in which the final Assault
> Differential is minus four or worse expends only one CP to defend.

### Procedure, restated

11.25's rule is: **the CP charge for a Combat Segment is a property of the HEX (or more precisely,
the Friendly formation co-located in that hex), not of the individual unit that pulled the trigger.**
Concretely:
1. If ANY unit belonging to a Friendly formation in a hex participates in combat this segment
   (Barrages, undergoes Barrage, fires Anti-Armor, Close Assaults, defends against Close Assault,
   Holds Off, etc.), then EVERY unit of that formation *in that same hex* is charged the applicable
   CP (5 if Phasing / 3 if non-Phasing, modulated by 11.23/11.27's lower rates only for the units that
   actually did the lower-rate thing) — **including units the player deliberately withheld from that
   specific action.**
2. The worked examples make the scope explicit: withholding one battalion (1st KRRC) from a
   Division-wide Close Assault does not exempt it from the Division's CP charge. Artillery alone
   barraging, with the rest of the Division doing nothing else that segment, still charges the WHOLE
   co-located Division 5 CP — not just the guns.
3. 11.26/11.27 clarify the two carve-outs to the flat 5-Phasing/3-non-Phasing rate (Probe at 2 CP, and
   the 11.27 "expends only 1 CP to defend" case for a defender crushed at Assault Differential ≤ −4) —
   but these lower rates are themselves also hex-wide per 11.25's principle: if the unit(s) that fought
   at the lower rate are what triggers 11.25's charge, the co-located withheld units presumably inherit
   that SAME lower rate (the rulebook's examples don't test this edge explicitly, but 11.26's Probe
   sentence — "if units in the hex WITH the Probing units Barrage the Enemy units those units expend 5
   CP's, regardless of the Probe attempt" — implies the charge tracks whichever combat activity
   actually happened in the hex; a hex with ONLY a Probe and nothing else charges the whole hex the
   Probe's 2 CP, but a hex with a Probe AND a Barrage charges the whole hex 5 CP because the Barrage
   is present).

### Already in the engine / gap assessment

`game/engine.py::_charge_combat_cp` (line ~3864, docstring: *"charge a unit its combat CP ONCE per
Combat Segment... a Phasing unit pays the 5-CP Assault, a Non-Phasing unit the 3-CP defence"*) is
called from `_barrage_step` (line ~4036), `_anti_armor_step` (line ~4106), and twice more in the Close
Assault path (lines ~4179, ~4194) — **in every call site, `_charge_combat_cp(r, side, u, charged)` is
invoked per participating unit `u`**, deduplicated via the `charged: set[str]` ledger so a unit that
touches multiple combat sub-phases in one segment (e.g. Barrages then Close Assaults) is charged only
once (correctly implementing 11.22's "can never expend more than five CP's in a given Combat"/11.24's
segment cap) — **but there is no step that extends the charge to co-located units of the same formation
that did NOT participate at all.** A withheld unit (like the 1st KRRC in the book's own example) is
simply never passed to `_charge_combat_cp` and so is never charged — this is a faithfulness gap
against 11.25. Confirmed by inspection; no counter-evidence found (no hex-wide sweep anywhere in
`_combat`, `_barrage_step`, `_anti_armor_step`, or the Close Assault resolution functions).

**11.21-11.24 and 11.26-11.27's flat-rate schedule (5/3, with 2 for Probe and 3 for undergoing
Barrage) is faithfully wired** via `data/cp_costs.json`'s `phasing_unit`/`non_phasing_unit` block
(`barrage_or_assault: 5`, `undergo_barrage: 3`, `probe: 2`, `barrage_or_defend: 3`, `defend_probe: 2`)
and `game/cp_costs.py::assault_cost`. The gap is narrowly 11.25's "spreads to the whole hex" clause —
the per-unit rate table is correct, the *scope* of who gets charged it is too narrow.

Implementation will need: after resolving which units in a hex actually fought (or Held Off, or
otherwise triggered a combat CP charge) this Combat Segment, identify every OTHER Friendly combat unit
co-located in that same hex belonging to the same participating side, and charge them the SAME rate
the participating unit(s) triggered (5/3 in the ordinary case; the lower Probe/defend-at-1 rates in the
narrower cases per the 11.26 reasoning above) via the existing `_spend_cp`/`CP_EXPENDED` channel — this
looks like a natural post-pass over `charged` per hex rather than a rewrite of the per-phase call
sites, but that is a design decision for the implementation phase, not this transcription.
