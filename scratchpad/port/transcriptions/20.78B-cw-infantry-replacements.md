## 2. What this table actually is — a random production stream, not a fixed schedule

**This is not a date-indexed arrival list.** Unlike the Reinforcement Track (whole units
arriving on named Game-Turns) or the [20.78C] Commonwealth Production Chart (tanks/guns,
each with a fixed campaign-wide `#` total and a `Max`-per-turn cap that the Commonwealth
Player draws down at will, Axis-Pool style), Infantry (and Truck) production is
**randomized every turn** per **[20.73]**, quoted verbatim (PDF p.032):

> **[20.73]** The Commonwealth Production System functions like the Axis Pools except for
> Infantry and Truck Points. The Player uses a random dice roll to determine what Infantry
> and Truck Points he receives per turn, depending upon the date of arrival.

Reading "per turn" together with the chart's own **Game-Turn**-indexed column structure
(not OpStage-indexed) and **[20.74]**'s instruction to spread a turn's total across
OpStages (see §4 below, which would be redundant if the roll already happened per
OpStage): the intended procedure is **one 2d6 roll every Game-Turn**, looked up against
whichever of the four GT-range columns contains that Game-Turn, yielding that Game-Turn's
Infantry RP output. There is **no player choice of column** — the column is simply "which
range does the current Game-Turn fall into," and no player choice of *whether* to roll —
production happens every turn the table is in effect (GT 3 through GT 107; GT 1–2 and GT
108–111 are outside all four ranges and produce none). **Flagged for the implementation
phase:** the rule text does not explicitly say the roll is *mandatory* every turn (as
opposed to something the CW player elects to draw, Axis-Pool style, whenever convenient);
I believe "receives... per turn" read against 20.74's forced OpStage-split is the stronger
reading, but the book does not spell out an explicit "MUST roll" imperative the way [20.62]
does for the Axis ("may bring in *any* number... during any one OpStage"). This is a
judgment call for the engine's `supply_orders()`/production step, not a chart-value
dispute — noted, not resolved by me.

## 3. Arrival lead time — four Game-Turns, confirmed three independent ways

1. **[20.78B]'s own footnote** (above): "arrive four Game-Turns later."
2. **[20.21]**, verbatim (PDF p.031): *"Replacement Points from Commonwealth Production
   also arrive in particular Stages as determined by the Commonwealth Player; these must
   be determined **four Game-Turns in advance** (see Case 20.7)."*
3. Contrast with the Axis: **[20.63]** requires Axis Pool replacements to be planned **two
   full Game-Turns** in advance — the Commonwealth's Infantry lead time is **double** the
   Axis's, on top of the Commonwealth having no tonnage/shipping cost at all (**[20.75]**:
   *"The Commonwealth Player has no Shipping Problems; his Replacement Points simply
   arrive"* — contrast **[20.62]**'s Axis tonnage charge, e.g. 10 Italian Infantry RPs = 350
   tons of scarce convoy shipping, subject to Malta interdiction).

**OWNER RULING NEEDED — flagging a genuine textual tension, not resolving it:** **[20.72]**
(PDF p.032), the *general* Commonwealth Production planning rule, states production must be
planned **"two months in advance"**:

> **[20.72]** The Commonwealth Player must plan *two* months in advance (unless specified
> in a given Scenario) for the arrival of Replacement Points via Production. Thus, for
> Points required for August, 1942, the Commonwealth Player must plan — using the
> Production Systems — in the first Naval Convoy Schedule Phase of **July**, 1942. However,
> the Commonwealth Player always uses the Production Table for the month (one month[s] in
> the future) in which he wishes his units to arrive — not the month in which he is
> consulting it.

> **[VERIFIER CORRECTION, 2026-07-23]** The transcriber's original blockquote of [20.72]
> was truncated at "...Naval Convoy Schedule Phase of July, 1942." — it dropped the rule's
> entire final sentence ("However, the Commonwealth Player always uses the Production Table
> for the month (one month in the future)... — not the month in which he is consulting
> it."), now restored above from a fresh 300dpi render of PDF p.032 (middle column). This
> omission mattered: the dropped sentence is the disambiguating clause for the exact
> contradiction flagged below. Read together, [20.72] describes a **two-step** timing —
> consult the table *dated one month ahead* of the consulting month, and plan *two months*
> ahead of arrival — which is a plausible reconciliation the Reading-A/B analysis below did
> not have in front of it. The italics correction: the book italicizes only the word *two*
> (not "two months"). The book prints "one month**s**" (a printer's typo for "one month");
> the right margin of the scan clips the final letter, but the OCR and the 90-charts
> transcription both read "months", so it is quoted "one month[s]" here. The OWNER RULING
> flag stands, but the owner now has the complete rule text to rule on.

The worked example inside this very sentence (plan in July for August arrival) only
demonstrates **one month** of lead, not two — and a Game-Turn is one week (111 Game-Turns ≈
the ~2.25-year Sept'40–Dec'42 span; [20.78's] own "four Game-Turns later" = four weeks = one
month), so [20.21]'s and [20.78B]'s explicit "**four** Game-Turns" is internally consistent
with a **one-month** lead — matching the worked example, but contradicting [20.72]'s stated
"**two months**." Two readings, both on the page, not reconciled by the book:
  - **Reading A** (favored by me, not authoritative): [20.72] is loose general prose
    describing the *default* Commonwealth Production lead time (which may genuinely be two
    months for the non-randomized [20.78C] equipment items, e.g. tanks/guns, which [20.73]
    explicitly carves Infantry/Truck *out of* — "functions like the Axis Pools **except
    for** Infantry and Truck Points"). Under this reading, [20.78C] equipment uses a
    ~2-month lead and Infantry/Truck use the specifically-stated 4-Game-Turn (~1-month)
    lead, and there is no contradiction — just two different lead times for two different
    production families, with [20.72]'s own worked example being sloppy prose.
  - **Reading B**: [20.72] states the lead time as two months, full stop, for *all*
    Commonwealth Production including Infantry, and [20.21]/[20.78B]'s "four Game-Turns" is
    the error (perhaps a mis-set number that should read "eight").
  - Pages: [20.72] is PDF p.032; [20.21] is PDF p.031; [20.78B]'s footnote is PDF p.141. I
    read all three directly off the scan (not OCR) and each is unambiguous on its own page
    — the ambiguity is only in reconciling them against each other.

I default to Reading A for any engine implementation of *this* table (four Game-Turns,
consistent between [20.21] and [20.78B] and matching the worked example's arithmetic), but
this needs an explicit owner call before [20.78C]'s equipment lead time is coded, since that
chart carries no lead-time footnote of its own and inherits whatever [20.72] means.

## 4. Where the Infantry RPs enter, and how they're split

**[20.76]**, verbatim (PDF p.032): *"All Planned Replacements arrive in any hex in Cairo or
Alexandria. They, like other Replacements, must undergo Training (see Case 20.43)."*

**[20.78B]'s footnote adds a same-Game-Turn grouping constraint** not present for other
replacement types: *"the infantry Replacement Points arriving during a Game-Turn must all
be placed in the same city"* — i.e. the Commonwealth Player picks Cairo **or** Alexandria
for that Game-Turn's whole Infantry batch, not a hex-by-hex or split distribution (contrast
[20.14], which lets Reinforcement-Track units and returning Withdrawals go to either city
"at the Commonwealth Player's option" with no such single-city constraint for a given
Game-Turn's cohort).

**Training** (already fully transcribed at
`scratchpad/port/transcriptions/17.3-20.43-training.md`, cross-referenced not duplicated
here): Infantry Replacement Points train for **three OpStages** ([20.43]'s table, PDF
p.031, confirmed against the render), at any of the six Commonwealth training locations
listed in **[17.32]** — Cairo (any city hex), Helwan (hex 1430), Alexandria (any city hex),
or Amiriya/Abouqir (per the sibling doc) — which is a **superset** of the two arrival
cities. So: RPs *arrive* only at Cairo or Alexandria (one city per Game-Turn's batch, per
above), but may then be moved to Helwan/Amiriya/Abouqir to train if the Commonwealth Player
chooses, before being trucked to the front per **[20.44]**.

**[20.74]**, verbatim (PDF p.032) — governs how a Game-Turn's total production (all types,
not just Infantry) is spread across that Game-Turn's OpStages: *"Replacement Points
scheduled for any given turn must be divided among the OpStages as evenly as possible. This
refers to the total Points for all types. Thus the Commonwealth Player may bring 10 Medium
Trucks in one OpStage and 10 Infantry Points in the next."* Note the example shows
**whole-type-per-OpStage** splitting (all 10 trucks in one stage, all 10 infantry in the
next), i.e. "evenly as possible" is evaluated over the *combined point count across types*
per OpStage, not that every individual type must itself be fractionally divided.

## 6. Expected total across the campaign — corroborates the port plan's "~1,617"

The table has no explicit campaign-total cap (unlike [20.78C], which prints a `#` = total
column per item). Since production is a 2d6 roll every Game-Turn from GT3–107 (see §2), I
computed the **expected value** using standard 2d6 probabilities (P(2)=P(12)=1/36,
P(3)=P(11)=2/36, P(4)=P(10)=3/36, P(5)=P(9)=4/36, P(6)=P(8)=5/36, P(7)=6/36), to sanity-check
against `scratchpad/port/00-THE-PORT-PLAN.md:900`'s "Expected yield ≈1,617 Infantry
Replacement Points across the campaign":

| GT range | # GTs | column EV (RP/turn) | range subtotal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 3...30 | 28 | 216/36 = 6.000 | 168.00 |
| 31...46 | 16 | 503/36 = 13.972 | 223.56 |
| 47...102 | 56 | 771/36 = 21.417 | 1,199.33 |
| 103...107 | 5 | 180/36 = 5.000 | 25.00 |
| **Total** | **105** | | **≈1,615.9** |

This lands within ~1 point of the port plan's "~1,617" figure (small gap consistent with
rounding in whichever quick estimate produced that number) — **strong corroboration that
both the table transcription and the "one 2d6 roll per Game-Turn, GT3–107" mechanic read in
§2 are correct.** Note GT3–107 (105 turns) plus the six uncovered turns (GT1–2, GT108–111)
sums to exactly 111 = the full campaign length, and GT107 + the 4-Game-Turn lead time = 111
exactly — **the table's last productive column is deliberately sized so the last possible
roll's replacements land on the last possible Game-Turn.** This is a real structural
feature of the design, not a coincidence, and is worth preserving in any engine port (i.e.
don't extend the roll window past GT107, and don't let arrivals run past GT111).

**Caveat:** this ≈1,616 is an *expectation*, not a guaranteed total — actual per-seed output
varies with the RNG. The theoretical extremes are wide: rolling the "none" cell every turn
where one exists (columns A, B, D each have two "none" rows; column C has none — every 2d6
result in the GT47–102 column produces a positive number) drives the total arbitrarily low
over a large-deviation run, while rolling each column's best cell every turn caps it at
28×20 + 16×25 + 56×35 + 5×13 = 560 + 400 + 1,960 + 65 = **2,985**. No campaign-total cap is
printed for this table the way [20.78C] prints one — if the engine wants a hard ceiling for
balance/determinism reasons, that would be an invented number, not a transcribed one, and
must be flagged as such per the port's own "never invent" rule if ever added.

## 7. Summary for the implementation phase

- **Mechanism:** one 2d6 roll per Game-Turn, GT3 through GT107 inclusive (105 rolls total
  across a full campaign); look up the roll against whichever of the four GT-range columns
  contains the current Game-Turn; the resulting number is that Game-Turn's Infantry RP
  output, "planned" that turn.
- **Lead time:** arrives exactly **4 Game-Turns later** ([20.21], [20.78B] footnote,
  consistent with each other; contradicts [20.72]'s "two months" — see §3 flag).
- **Destination:** the Commonwealth Player's choice of **Cairo or Alexandria**, one city for
  that whole Game-Turn's Infantry batch ([20.76], [20.78B] footnote).
- **OpStage split:** divided as evenly as possible among that (arrival) Game-Turn's
  OpStages, pooled together with that turn's other replacement-type arrivals ([20.74]).
- **Post-arrival:** must Train 3 OpStages ([20.43]) at Cairo/Alexandria/Helwan/Amiriya/
  Abouqir ([17.32], not duplicated here — see `17.3-20.43-training.md`) before being
  trucked to the front and absorbed ([20.44], [20.49]).
- **No shipping/tonnage cost** ([20.75]) — unlike every Axis Replacement Point.
- **Unmodified** in both campaign scenarios ([64.54.B]).
- **Nothing in `game/` currently reads or models any of this** — confirmed by grep, §0.
