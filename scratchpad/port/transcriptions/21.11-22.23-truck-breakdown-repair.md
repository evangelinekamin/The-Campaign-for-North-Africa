## 2. Rule 22.21-22.28 — FIELD REPAIRS, verbatim, 22.23 is the truck column
(docs/rules/22-repair.md, scan pp. 034-035)

> **[22.21]** Players may attempt to repair Broken down vehicles in the field. Destroyed
> tanks may not be repaired in the field; they must be towed to a Facility (exceptions:
> see Cases 22.6 and 22.7).
>
> **[22.22]** Field Repairs may take place in any hex in which there is a Broken down
> vehicle stacked with a Friendly unit of any type, even a Squadron Ground Support Unit
> (see Case 22.13a). Field Repairs may not be made if the vehicle in question has been
> towed in that Repair Phase (see also Case 22.6).
>
> **[22.23]** To repair trucks in the field, the Player rolls **one die** for Broken down
> trucks in the hex in question. If he rolls a **2** he may repair **one** Light or one
> Medium Truck Point. If he rolls a **1** he may repair **two** Light or two Medium Trucks
> (or any combination of Light and Medium equal to two) or **one Heavy Truck**. Field
> repair of Trucks requires **no expenditure of supplies**.
>
> **[22.24]** To field repair armored cars and/or Recce TOE Strength Points, the Player
> rolls one die for all the A/C's and Recce points in the hex. If he rolls a 1 he may
> repair one such TOE Strength Point. No supply expenditure is required for field repair
> of Armored Recce or Armored car points.
>
> **[22.25]** To field repair tanks or self-propelled artillery the Player consults the
> Broken Down Vehicle Repair Table (22.8) under the column for "Field Repair." ... All
> fractions are rounded up, unless there is only one TOE point undergoing repair. In such
> case the Player ignores the 10% result.
>
> **[22.26]** For each tank TOE Strength Point to undergo field repairs, the Player must
> have present in the hex and expend before rolling for repairs one Fuel Point. ...
>
> **[22.27]** Weather affects Field Repairs; see Case 22.13d.
>
> **[22.28]** Players will note that field repairs are a risky proposition.

Scan-verified word-for-word on p. 035 — no discrepancy, matches
`docs/rules/22-repair.md` lines 34-52 exactly.

**Key facts for the truck column specifically:**
- die roll of 1 rolls higher yield than 2 (the table is *inverted* relative to intuition —
  low roll good). 1 → **2 points**; 2 → **1 point**; 3-6 → **0**.
- Truck field repair costs **zero supplies** — unlike tank field repair (1 Fuel/TOE,
  22.26) or facility repair (1 Fuel + 1 Stores, 22.35).
- "Heavy Truck Point" repair only ever happens on the die-1 result, and only as an
  *alternative* to "two Light or Medium" — you cannot partially repair a Heavy Truck.

Also verified 22.13 (the four repair-blocking conditions: enemy-controlled hex unless a
Major Repair Facility, no supplies, towed-this-phase, Rainstorm/Sandstorm weather except
at a Major Repair Facility) applies identically to trucks — nothing truck-specific is
exempted.

**NEW FLAG — the ‡ footnote's worked example, verbatim off the scan (p. 109, bottom of
the `[54.2]` box). This is the COMPLETE, un-truncated sentence, read whole off a 300dpi
render of the footnote line (the `[54.2]` chart is upright at the bottom of p. 109, NOT
rotated):**

> ‡Light Trucks, if not being moved along a Road, garner one break down point in addition
> to those acquired by entering the hex or crossing the hexside. e.g., moving along a
> track one hex across a wadi into a rough hex accumulates **18 BP's** (½ × 8 for the
> wadi + ½ × 8 for the rough + 1 each for the wadi hexside and the rough hex).

**The scan unambiguously prints "18 BP's" as two full-size digits — but the FULL
parenthetical is also printed, entirely legible, and sums to 10:** ½×8 (wadi) + ½×8
(rough) + 1 (wadi hexside off-road) + 1 (rough hex off-road) = 4 + 4 + 1 + 1 = **10**,
not 18. The headline number and its own explanatory arithmetic — both fully printed in
the same ruled box — flatly contradict each other. This is a genuine **book misprint of
exactly the 54.17 class** CLAUDE.md already documents (a printed value that disagrees
with itself on the page).

Note the shape of the error: **18 is exactly the UN-halved sum** — 8 (wadi hexside) + 8
(rough hex) + 1 + 1 = 18 — whereas **10 is the same sum with the track's ½ applied to
both terrain values**, which is precisely what the parenthetical writes out ("½ × 8 …
½ × 8 …"). So the two readings are not arbitrary: 18 = "a track does NOT halve BV",
10 = "a track DOES halve BV". Rule 8.37 note 8 and the parenthetical itself both say the
track halves — which independently favours Reading A (10). The most likely authoring
story is that the headline 18 was computed before the ½-track rule was applied and never
updated to match the corrected parenthetical.

## 6. Implementation status in `game/` (read-only observation — I did not touch `game/`
or `data/`; another workflow owns those right now)

Confirmed the task brief's claim ("trucks currently never break down") and localized
exactly why, for the next implementation phase:

- **The chart data is already fully correct and already ported.**
  `game/combat_tables.py::_FIELD_REPAIR["truck"] = {0:2, 1:2, 2:1, 3:0, 4:0, 5:0, 6:0}`
  matches §3c exactly. `game/combat_tables.py::breakdown_band/_column/_result` correctly
  implement the 21.38 table from `data/breakdown_rates.json`. Truck BAR (2L) is recorded
  in `data/breakdown_rates.json::bar_by_model.trucks_54_2` but is **not yet wired into
  `game/oob.py`'s per-model BAR block** (`game/oob.py:807` mirrors only tank/AC/SP-gun
  BARs today, per its own comment "`[21.12]/[4.47-4.49]`").
- **The gate that excludes trucks:** `game/state.py`'s `Unit.breaks_down` property
  returns `self.is_armor` (`armor_protection > 0`). Truck Points are never `Unit`
  instances, so this property never sees them. Both `game/engine.py::_breakdown()` (the
  21.24 check-for-breakdown loop) and `game/engine.py::_repair()` (the 22.12 field-repair
  loop) iterate `r.state.living(side)` (`Unit`s) filtered on `.breaks_down` — trucks are
  structurally invisible to both loops.
- **Where truck state actually lives, and what it's missing:** first-line trucks are
  `Unit.fl_light/fl_medium/fl_heavy` — bare carrying-capacity integers attached to a
  parent combat `Unit` (rule 53.11), with **no** per-class `bar`, `bp_accumulated`,
  `bp_checked_column` or `broken_down` fields at all (contrast `Unit`'s tank/AC/SP-gun
  fields at `game/state.py:100-108`, which have exactly these four). 2nd/3rd-line trucks
  are `TruckFormation` (`game/state.py:489`, fields `id/side/hex/truck_class/points/line`
  plus four cargo-pool ints) — also with no breakdown-tracking fields whatsoever.
  `game/movement.py::breakdown_points()` already computes the correct BP-per-step for a
  motorized mover (terrain + hexside, track-halving, the light-truck off-road +1/+1 note),
  but nothing currently accumulates that total against a truck-shaped `bp_accumulated`,
  because no such field exists to accumulate it into.
- **What this means for the eventual fix:** the missing piece is not a chart or a rule
  reading — it is a data-model decision (does each of `fl_light/fl_medium/fl_heavy`
  need its own `bar/bp_accumulated/bp_checked_column/broken_down` shadow-state the way a
  `Unit`'s tank/AC/SP-gun TOE does, or does the "one Truck Point = one TOE Strength Point"
  equivalence of 21.34 mean the simplest-possible move is to treat first-line trucks as
  three synthetic per-class sub-units for breakdown-checking purposes only? Same question
  for `TruckFormation`.) That decision is out of scope for a transcription pass and is
  exactly the kind of design call CLAUDE.md reserves for "stop and ask" / the implementation
  phase, not this one.
